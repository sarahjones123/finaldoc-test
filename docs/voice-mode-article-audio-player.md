<h1>Voice Mode and Article Audio Player</h1>
<p>FinalDoc includes two audio features for your knowledge base: the <strong>Article Audio Player</strong> (text-to-speech for articles) and <strong>Voice Mode</strong> (live voice conversation with the AI assistant). Both are powered by OpenAI's speech technology.</p>

<h2>Article Audio Player</h2>
<p>The Audio Player lets readers listen to articles instead of reading them — useful for people on the go, those with accessibility needs, or anyone who prefers audio content.</p>

<h3>Enabling the Listen Button</h3>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → Appearance → Article Display</strong></li>
<li>Find <strong>Show Listen Button</strong> and toggle it ON</li>
</ol>
<p>A "Listen" button (with a play icon and headphones) now appears at the top of every article on your public knowledge base.</p>

<h3>How Readers Use It</h3>
<ol>
<li>Reader opens an article on the public KB</li>
<li>Clicks the <strong>Listen</strong> button at the top</li>
<li>An audio player bar appears at the bottom of the screen</li>
<li>The article text is read aloud by an AI voice</li>
<li>Reader can: Play/Pause, Skip forward/back 15 seconds, Adjust volume, Change playback speed (0.75x, 1x, 1.25x, 1.5x, 2x)</li>
</ol>

<h3>Voice</h3>
<p>The audio uses OpenAI's text-to-speech (TTS) with the "nova" voice by default — a natural, warm female voice. Configure the voice in <strong>Settings → AI (Ved AI) → Reader Chatbot → Voice Settings</strong>.</p>

<h2>Voice Mode (AI Conversation)</h2>
<p>Voice Mode lets readers have a live spoken conversation with the AI assistant — they speak, the AI listens and responds in speech. This goes beyond the text chatbot — it is a real-time two-way voice conversation.</p>

<h3>Enabling Voice Mode</h3>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → Appearance → Portal Builder → AI Chatbot Button Style</strong></li>
<li>Select <strong>Capsule</strong> as the button style</li>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → AI (Ved AI) → Reader Chatbot → Voice Settings</strong></li>
<li>Enable <strong>Voice Mode</strong></li>
</ol>
<p>The chatbot button changes to a split Capsule pill with "Voice" on one side and "Chat" on the other, with an animated orb in the center.</p>

<h3>How Readers Use Voice Mode</h3>
<ol>
<li>Reader clicks the <strong>Voice</strong> side of the Capsule button</li>
<li>A voice interface opens — the AI introduces itself</li>
<li>Reader speaks their question naturally (microphone permission required)</li>
<li>The AI responds in speech in real time</li>
<li>The conversation continues back and forth naturally</li>
<li>Click End to close the voice session</li>
</ol>

<h3>Available Voices</h3>
<p>Choose from 6 OpenAI voices: <strong>alloy</strong>, <strong>echo</strong>, <strong>shimmer</strong>, <strong>nova</strong>, <strong>coral</strong>, <strong>ash</strong>. Click Preview next to each voice to hear a sample before selecting. Configure in <strong>Settings → AI (Ved AI) → Reader Chatbot → Voice Settings</strong>.</p>

<h3>Browser Requirements</h3>
<p>Voice Mode requires:</p>
<ul>
<li>A modern browser (Chrome 90+, Edge 90+, Firefox 88+, or Safari 14+)</li>
<li>Microphone access permission (readers are prompted on first use)</li>
<li>A stable internet connection (Voice Mode uses WebSocket for real-time audio)</li>
</ul>
