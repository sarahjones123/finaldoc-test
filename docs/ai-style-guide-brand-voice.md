<h1>AI Style Guide and Brand Voice</h1>
<p>The Style Guide tells the AI how to write — in your brand's voice, tone, and terminology. When you define style rules, all AI-generated content (in the Writer Assistant, the Reader Chatbot, and the AI Optimizer recommendations) follows your guidelines automatically.</p>

<h2>Opening the Style Guide</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → AI (Ved AI) → AI Style Guide &amp; Brand Voice</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>What to Define in Your Style Guide</h2>

<h3>Brand Voice</h3>
<p>Describe how your brand communicates. Examples:</p>
<ul>
<li>"Write in a friendly, approachable tone. Avoid jargon. Assume readers are non-technical"</li>
<li>"Use a professional, authoritative tone. We speak to developers and technical professionals"</li>
<li>"Keep responses concise and direct. Get to the point quickly. No fluff"</li>
</ul>

<h3>Grammar and Style Rules</h3>
<p>Specific writing rules to enforce. Examples:</p>
<ul>
<li>"Always use active voice. Never use passive voice"</li>
<li>"Use 'you' to address the reader, not 'users' or 'customers'"</li>
<li>"Write in American English (not British English)"</li>
<li>"Numbers 1–9 are written as words (one, two...). Numbers 10 and above use numerals"</li>
<li>"Do not start sentences with 'However'"</li>
</ul>

<h3>Terminology</h3>
<p>Approved terms and terms to avoid. Examples:</p>
<ul>
<li>"The product is called 'FinalDoc', not 'Finaldoc', 'Final Doc', or 'finaldoc'"</li>
<li>"Use 'knowledge base' (two words, lowercase) not 'KB' unless abbreviated in a table"</li>
<li>"Call it a 'workspace' not a 'project version' or 'space'"</li>
</ul>

<h3>Formatting Rules</h3>
<p>Preferred content structure guidelines. Examples:</p>
<ul>
<li>"Use numbered lists for procedures, bullet lists for features or options"</li>
<li>"Every how-to article must start with the prerequisite and end with a 'What to do next' section"</li>
<li>"Code examples must include a comment explaining each key line"</li>
</ul>

<h2>How It Affects AI Output</h2>
<p>The style guide is included in the AI system prompt for every request. The AI reads the rules before generating any content and tries to follow them. More specific rules give better results — vague rules like "write well" have little effect.</p>

<h2>Saving and Activating</h2>
<p>Type your style guide in the text area provided (plain text or Markdown). Click <strong>Save Style Guide</strong>. The style guide is immediately active for all new AI requests — existing generated content is not retroactively changed.</p>

<h2>Testing Your Style Guide</h2>
<p>Use the <strong>Writer Assistant</strong> in the Documentation editor to test your style guide immediately. Ask the AI to write a paragraph about any topic and see if it follows your rules. Adjust the style guide if the output does not match your expectations.</p>
