<h1>Search Analytics &amp; Content Gap Analysis</h1>
<p>The Search Analytics dashboard shows what your readers are searching for on your knowledge base. This is some of the most valuable data you can have: readers who search are telling you exactly what they need. When searches return no results, you've found a content gap — a topic your KB doesn't cover yet.</p>

<h2>Opening Search Analytics</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Search Analytics &amp; Content Gap Analysis</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>The Search Terms Table</h2>
<p>The main table lists every search query made on your KB, with:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Search query</strong> — The exact words the reader typed</li>
<li><strong>Search count</strong> — How many times this query was searched</li>
<li><strong>Results returned</strong> — How many articles matched this query</li>
<li><strong>Zero results?</strong> — Highlighted if this query consistently returned no results</li>
<li><strong>Last searched</strong> — When this query was last made</li>
</ul>

<h2>Content Gaps Tab (Zero-Result Searches)</h2>
<p>Click the <strong>Content Gaps</strong> tab to filter down to only searches that returned zero results. These are your highest-priority documentation opportunities — readers are actively looking for content you haven't written yet.</p>
<p>For each gap, you can:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>AI Auto-Draft</strong> — Uses AI to generate a full draft article covering this topic. The draft is created in Documentation and ready for you to review and publish</li>
<li><strong>Create Article</strong> — Opens a new blank article with the search query pre-filled as the title</li>
<li><strong>Dismiss</strong> — Mark as not relevant (e.g., spam queries or things outside your product's scope)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Top Searches</h2>
<p>The <strong>Top Searches</strong> section shows your most-searched queries ranked by frequency. These tell you what your readers care about most. If you have articles on these topics, check that they rank well in search results. If they don't show up, optimize the article title and description to include the search terms readers use.</p>

<h2>Search Trend Chart</h2>
<p>A line chart shows total daily search volume over time. Spikes often correlate with:</p>
<ul>
<li>Product releases (readers looking for new feature docs)</li>
<li>Incidents or outages (readers looking for status/workarounds)</li>
<li>Marketing campaigns bringing new visitors with different questions</li>
<li>Seasonal business patterns</li>
</ul>

<h2>Date Range Filter</h2>
<p>Use the date range picker at the top to analyze search data for a specific time period: last 7 days, last 30 days, last 90 days, or a custom range.</p>

<h2>AI-Powered Semantic Fallback</h2>
<p>When a reader searches and the standard search returns zero results, FinalDoc automatically runs a semantic (vector-based) search as a fallback. This finds articles that are about the same topic even if the exact words don't match — and shows them to the reader as suggestions. This means some "zero result" searches may still result in readers finding what they need via the AI fallback. Your Search Analytics still records these as zero-result queries so you can see what readers originally typed.</p>

<h2>Using Content Gap Data to Prioritize Writing</h2>
<ol>
<li>Sort by <strong>Search Count</strong> descending in the Content Gaps tab</li>
<li>The query searched most often with zero results is your highest-priority documentation gap</li>
<li>Click <strong>AI Auto-Draft</strong> to get a full first draft in seconds</li>
<li>Review, add your product-specific details, and publish</li>
<li>Monitor: Over the next few weeks, the search count for that query should shift from "zero results" to returning results</li>
</ol>
