<h1>AI Optimizer Deep Dive</h1>
<p>The AI Optimizer (formerly Doc Optimizer) analyzes your articles for quality, clarity, completeness, SEO, and readability — then gives you specific, actionable suggestions to improve them. Think of it as an intelligent editor that reads your articles with fresh eyes and points out what could be better.</p>

<h2>Opening the AI Optimizer</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Toolbox</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>AI Optimizer</strong> (under AI Tools)</li>
</ol>

<h2>Analyzing an Article</h2>
<ol>
<li>In the AI Optimizer, click <strong>Select Article</strong></li>
<li>Choose an article from the dropdown (or search by title)</li>
<li>Click <strong>Analyze Article</strong></li>
<li>Wait 10–30 seconds while the AI reviews the article</li>
</ol>

<h2>The Analysis Report</h2>
<p>The report has several sections:</p>

<h3>Overall Quality Score</h3>
<p>A score from 0–100 rating the article's overall documentation quality. The score is based on a weighted average of the checks below. Aim for 70+ for good documentation, 85+ for excellent.</p>

<h3>Readability</h3>
<ul>
<li>Flesch-Kincaid reading level — Is the article at the right level for your audience?</li>
<li>Sentence length — Are sentences too long on average?</li>
<li>Passive voice % — High passive voice usage makes content harder to follow</li>
<li>Jargon density — Are too many technical terms unexplained?</li>
</ul>

<h3>Structure</h3>
<ul>
<li>Heading hierarchy — Are H2/H3 headings used appropriately?</li>
<li>Content length — Is the article too short (missing detail) or too long (needs splitting)?</li>
<li>Introduction — Does the article have a clear opening that explains what it covers?</li>
<li>Conclusion / next steps — Does the article end with clear guidance?</li>
</ul>

<h3>SEO</h3>
<ul>
<li>Title optimization — Is the title descriptive and keyword-rich?</li>
<li>Meta description — Is it set and at the right length?</li>
<li>Internal links — Does the article link to related articles?</li>
<li>Image alt text — Do all images have descriptive alt text?</li>
</ul>

<h3>Content Quality</h3>
<ul>
<li>Missing sections — Topics the AI expects to see (based on the article type) that are absent</li>
<li>Outdated language — References to outdated product terms or UI elements</li>
<li>Completeness — Does the article fully cover its stated topic?</li>
</ul>

<h2>Applying Suggestions</h2>
<p>Each suggestion includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>A description of the issue</li>
<li>The severity (Critical, Warning, or Suggestion)</li>
<li>Specific text from the article where the issue occurs (click to jump to it in the editor)</li>
<li>How to fix it</li>
</ul>
<p>Click <strong>Open Article</strong> to open the article for editing alongside the optimizer report.</p>

<h2>Bulk Analysis</h2>
<p>To analyze all articles at once:</p>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Analyze All Articles</strong></li>
<li>The optimizer runs through every published article and calculates quality scores</li>
<li>A table shows all articles sorted by quality score — lowest scores first</li>
<li>Start fixing the worst-scoring articles first</li>
</ol>
