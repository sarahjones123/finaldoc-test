<h1>Analytics Overview</h1>
<p>FinalDoc's Analytics section gives you deep insight into how readers use your knowledge base — which articles they read, how they find content, what they search for, and where they get stuck. Use these insights to improve your documentation and serve your readers better.</p>

<h2>Opening Analytics</h2>
<p>Click <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar. The main analytics dashboard loads with summary metrics at the top.</p>

<h2>Summary Metrics (Top of Dashboard)</h2>
<p>The dashboard header shows key numbers at a glance:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Total article views</strong> — How many times articles have been opened this period</li>
<li><strong>Unique readers</strong> — How many individual visitors read your KB</li>
<li><strong>Search queries</strong> — Total number of searches performed</li>
<li><strong>Average reading time</strong> — How long readers spend on articles on average</li>
<li><strong>Feedback score</strong> — Overall helpfulness rating from reader feedback</li>
</ul>

<h2>Analytics Sections</h2>

<h3>Article and Reader Analytics</h3>
<p>See which articles are most popular, which have the highest feedback scores, and which are generating the most time-on-page. Sort by views, time spent, feedback rating, or last updated date. Click any article to see its individual analytics dashboard.</p>

<h3>Reader Analytics</h3>
<p>If reader authentication is enabled, you can see per-reader data — which articles each reader has read, their reading progress, and their search history. Useful for customer success teams tracking onboarding progress.</p>

<h3>Search Analytics and Content Gaps</h3>
<p>See every search query entered in your knowledge base, how many results it returned, and whether readers found what they were looking for. Zero-result searches are highlighted — these are <strong>content gaps</strong>: topics your readers need help with but your KB does not cover yet.</p>
<p>Click any content gap to auto-generate a draft article on that topic using AI, or to manually create a new article targeted at that query.</p>

<h3>Activity Heatmap</h3>
<p>A visual calendar showing knowledge base activity over the past 12 months — similar to GitHub's contribution graph. Darker squares mean more reader activity that day. Hover over any day to see the exact number of views. Useful for spotting seasonal patterns and measuring the impact of content pushes.</p>

<h3>Contribution Timeline</h3>
<p>Shows team member activity — who created or updated articles, when, and how many. Helps managers see which team members are actively contributing to documentation.</p>

<h3>Content Health Dashboard</h3>
<p>An overview of your KB's overall quality:</p>
<ul>
<li>Articles with no description</li>
<li>Articles with no featured image</li>
<li>Articles not updated in more than 6 months (potentially stale)</li>
<li>Articles with low feedback scores</li>
<li>Articles with no tags</li>
</ul>
<p>Click any category to see the specific articles that need attention.</p>

<h3>Team Performance Metrics</h3>
<p>Shows team-level statistics: articles created per team member, edit frequency, review completion rates, and approval flow performance. Useful for team leads doing performance reviews or planning documentation sprints.</p>

<h3>Links and Broken Link Checking</h3>
<p>Scans all your articles for broken links — links to external URLs that no longer work (404 errors). Run a scan and get a list of every broken link with the article it appears in and the dead URL. Fix broken links before readers find them.</p>

<h2>Date Range Filter</h2>
<p>Most analytics views have a date range picker at the top. Select a custom date range or use presets: Last 7 days, Last 30 days, Last 90 days, Last 12 months. Comparing time periods helps you spot trends.</p>

<h2>Exporting Analytics Data</h2>
<p>Export analytics as a CSV file for use in spreadsheets or BI tools. Click the <strong>Export</strong> button in any analytics view to download a CSV of the data currently shown.</p>
