<h1>Reviewing AI Recommendations</h1>
<p>After running a Ticket Analysis, the AI produces specific recommendations — documentation improvements it thinks would reduce your support ticket volume. This article explains how to review, act on, and dismiss these recommendations.</p>

<h2>Finding Recommendations</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Ticket Analysis</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click the <strong>Recommendations</strong> tab</li>
</ol>
<p>Recommendations are sorted by estimated impact — the ones at the top are expected to deflect the most tickets if addressed.</p>

<h2>Types of Recommendations</h2>

<h3>Create New Article</h3>
<p>The AI identified a topic with significant ticket volume and no existing KB coverage.</p>
<p>Recommendation card shows:</p>
<ul>
<li>Suggested article title</li>
<li>Estimated ticket count this article would address</li>
<li>Sample tickets that illustrate the topic</li>
<li><strong>Create Draft</strong> button — opens a new article with an AI-generated draft</li>
</ul>

<h3>Update Existing Article</h3>
<p>The AI found tickets about a topic you have an article on — but readers are still asking the question, suggesting the article isn't answering it well enough.</p>
<p>Recommendation card shows:</p>
<ul>
<li>The existing article title (click to open it for editing)</li>
<li>What the AI thinks is missing (e.g., "Missing: steps for mobile app users", "Unclear: what happens after the 14-day trial ends")</li>
<li>Sample tickets showing what readers were confused about</li>

</ul>

<h3>Add FAQ Entry</h3>
<p>A very common, simple question that would be well served by a short FAQ article or FAQ section addition.</p>

<h2>Acting on Recommendations</h2>
<p>For each recommendation:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Accept &amp; Create Draft</strong> — AI generates a draft article. Opens in Documentation editor for you to review and finalize</li>
<li><strong>Accept &amp; Edit Existing</strong> — Opens the existing article in editor with the AI's notes on what to add</li>
<li><strong>Dismiss</strong> — Mark as not relevant. The recommendation disappears from your list (you can view dismissed recommendations by toggling a filter)</li>
<li><strong>Snooze</strong> — Hide for 30 days. Resurfaces automatically to check if you've addressed it</li>
</ul>

<h2>Tracking Progress</h2>
<p>As you act on recommendations, the dashboard shows:</p>
<ul>
<li>Total recommendations: X total, Y addressed, Z dismissed, W pending</li>
<li>Estimated tickets addressed — if all addressed recommendations were live, this many tickets might be self-served</li>
</ul>

<h2>Refreshing Recommendations</h2>
<p>After you've added or updated articles based on recommendations, run a new analysis to see if ticket volume on those topics has decreased. Over time, this feedback loop should show your KB deflecting more and more tickets.</p>
