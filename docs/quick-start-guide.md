<p>This guide provides a step-by-step walkthrough to help you quickly set up and publish your first knowledge base article in FinalDoc. By the end of this guide, you will have a basic knowledge base structure in place, along with a live article accessed by your readers.</p>
<h2>Step 1: Create a category</h2>
<p>Categories organise your articles like folders. Before you write an article, create at least one category.</p>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Documentation</strong> in the left sidebar.  </li>
<li>Look at the left panel. You will see your article tree. It will be empty initially.  </li>
<li>Click <strong>+ New category</strong> or the folder icon at the top of the panel.  </li>
<li>Enter a name for your category, for example: "Getting started", "User guide", or "API reference".  </li>
<li>Press <strong>Enter</strong> or click <strong>Save</strong>.</li>
</ol>
<p>Your category appears in the left panel. You can create multiple categories and nest them to form subcategories. Click the <strong>+</strong> icon inside a category to add a child category.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Step 2: Create your first article</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click the <strong>+</strong> button next to your category or click <strong>+ New article</strong>.  </li>
<li>Enter a clear and descriptive title, for example: "Install the application".  </li>
<li>The article opens in the editor. You will see a blank writing area in the center. This is where you write your content.</li>
</ol>
<hr />
<h2>Step 3: Write your article</h2>
<p>Click anywhere in the editor and start typing. The editor works like a standard word processor.</p>
<ul>
<li>Use the <strong>toolbar at the top</strong> to format text such as headings, bold, italic, lists, links, images, tables, and code blocks.  </li>
<li>Press <strong>Enter</strong> to create a new paragraph.  </li>
<li>Use keyboard shortcuts such as <strong>Ctrl+B</strong> for bold, <strong>Ctrl+I</strong> for italic, and <strong>Ctrl+K</strong> to insert a link.  </li>
<li>Paste content from Word, Google Docs, or websites. FinalDoc automatically cleans and formats the content.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Autosave is enabled.</strong> FinalDoc saves your work automatically every few seconds. A "Saved" indicator appears at the top of the editor. You do not need to manually save your work.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Step 4: Add a description and tags</h2>
<p>Use the <strong>Inspector panel</strong> on the right side of the editor to configure additional details.</p>
<ul>
<li>Add a <strong>description</strong>. This is a short summary shown in search results and article listings.  </li>
<li>Set a <strong>featured image</strong>. This appears as a thumbnail in article lists.  </li>
<li>Add <strong>tags</strong> to improve searchability and link related content.  </li>
<li>Review or update the <strong>reading time</strong>. This is calculated automatically but can be modified.</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h2>Step 5: Publish your article</h2>
<p>When your article is ready:</p>
<ol>
<li>Click the <strong>Publish</strong> button in the top-right corner.  </li>
<li>The article status changes from <strong>Draft</strong> to <strong>Published</strong>.  </li>
<li>The article becomes immediately available on your public knowledge base.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Example URL:</strong><br /><code>https://your-project.finaldoc.io</code></p>
<hr />
<h2>Step 6: View your knowledge base</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click the <strong>View Site</strong> button (usually a globe icon in the top bar or sidebar).  </li>
<li>Your public knowledge base opens in a new tab.  </li>
<li>Locate your category and article.  </li>
<li>Click the article to view it as your readers will see it.</li>
</ol>
<hr />
<h2>What to do next</h2>
<p>You have successfully published your first article. Continue building your knowledge base with the following actions.</p>
<ul>
<li>Create additional articles to expand your documentation.  </li>
<li>Customize the appearance in <strong>Settings → Appearance → Design Studio</strong>.  </li>
<li>Invite team members from <strong>Settings → Security &amp; Access → Team Management</strong>.  </li>
<li>Configure a custom domain in <strong>Settings → Domain &amp; SEO → Custom Domain</strong>.  </li>
<li>Enable the AI assistant in <strong>Settings → AI (Ved AI) → Reader Chatbot</strong>, so your readers can ask questions and get answers instantly.</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<p>You now have a live and accessible knowledge base. Continue adding content and refining your structure to create a comprehensive documentation experience.</p>
