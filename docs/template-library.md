<h1>Template Library</h1>
<p>The Template Library is a collection of pre-built article structures — starting points for common documentation types. Instead of starting from a blank page every time, pick a template, fill in your content, and you're done. You can also save any of your own articles as reusable templates.</p>

<h2>Opening the Template Library</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Toolbox</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Page Templates</strong> (under Content Blocks)</li>
</ol>

<h2>Using a Template</h2>
<ol>
<li>In the Page Templates view, click a template to preview it</li>
<li>The preview shows the full article structure with placeholder content</li>
<li>Click <strong>Use Template</strong> or <strong>Import Starter Template</strong></li>
<li>A new article opens in the editor with the template content</li>
<li>Replace the placeholder text with your actual content</li>
<li>Delete any sections from the template that don't apply to your article</li>
<li>Publish when ready</li>
</ol>

<h2>Creating an Article from a Template (Documentation Editor)</h2>
<ol>
<li>In Documentation view, click <strong>+ New Article</strong></li>
<li>When prompted, click <strong>Choose Template</strong></li>
<li>Browse the list of available templates</li>
<li>Click a template to preview its structure</li>
<li>Click <strong>Use This Template</strong></li>
<li>The article editor opens with the template content already filled in</li>
</ol>

<h2>Saving Your Own Articles as Templates</h2>
<ol>
<li>Open an article in the editor that you want to use as a template</li>
<li>Click the three-dot menu in the editor toolbar → <strong>Save as Template</strong></li>
<li>Give the template a name</li>
<li>Click <strong>Save</strong></li>
</ol>
<p>Your custom template appears in the Page Templates view under a <strong>My Templates</strong> section. All team members can use templates you create.</p>

<h2>Managing Templates</h2>
<p>In the Page Templates view:</p>
<ul>
<li>Click any custom template → <strong>Edit</strong> to update its content</li>
<li>Click → <strong>Delete</strong> to remove a custom template (existing articles created from it are not affected)</li>
<li>Click → <strong>Duplicate</strong> to make a copy of a template to modify</li>
</ul>
<p>Built-in starter templates cannot be deleted but can be imported and then customized.</p>
