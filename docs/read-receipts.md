<h1>Read Receipts</h1>
<p>Read Receipts let you see which specific readers have read specific articles. When a logged-in reader opens an article and scrolls to a certain depth, FinalDoc marks that article as "read" for them. You can use this for compliance documentation, training tracking, or to follow up with readers who haven't yet read important articles.</p>

<h2>Enabling Read Receipts</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → Appearance → Article Display</strong></li>
<li>Find <strong>Read Receipts</strong> and toggle it ON</li>
</ol>
<p>Read Receipts only track <strong>logged-in readers</strong>. Anonymous visitors are not tracked per-article because there's no consistent identity to associate with a read.</p>

<h2>What Counts as "Read"</h2>
<p>An article is marked as read when a logged-in reader scrolls past 80% of the article's content. Simply viewing the top of an article does not count as a read — the reader needs to actually read through most of it.</p>

<h2>Viewing Read Receipts</h2>
<h3>Per Article</h3>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Documentation</strong></li>
<li>Click any article to open it</li>
<li>In the right Inspector panel, click <strong>Read Receipts</strong></li>
<li>A list shows which readers have read this article and when</li>
</ol>

<h3>Per Reader</h3>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Analytics → Reader Analytics</strong></li>
<li>Click on a specific reader</li>
<li>The reader detail page shows all articles they've fully read</li>
</ol>

<h2>Use Cases</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Compliance training</strong> — Require employees (readers) to read specific policy documents and track completion</li>
<li><strong>Onboarding</strong> — Track new customer/employee onboarding progress through a reading checklist</li>
<li><strong>Announcements</strong> — Know which customers have read an important update article</li>
<li><strong>Product documentation</strong> — Ensure support teams have read the latest product documentation before going on shift</li>
</ul>

<h2>Sending Read Reminders</h2>
<p>For articles that specific readers must read, you can send a reminder email:</p>
<ol>
<li>Open the article's Read Receipts panel</li>
<li>Select readers who haven't read it yet</li>
<li>Click <strong>Send Reminder</strong></li>
<li>FinalDoc sends a brief email with a link to the article</li>
</ol>

<h2>Privacy Disclosure</h2>
<p>If your KB is public or semi-public, inform readers in your Privacy Policy that you track article read completion for logged-in accounts. This is a standard analytics feature but should be disclosed.</p>
