<h1>Content Review Reminders</h1>
<p>Documentation gets outdated. Product features change, prices change, team members change — and articles that were accurate six months ago may now contain wrong information. Content Review lets you set reminder schedules on articles so your team gets a heads-up when content needs to be checked and updated.</p>

<h2>Opening Content Review</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Toolbox</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Under <strong>Content</strong>, click <strong>Content Review</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Setting a Review Reminder on an Article</h2>
<ol>
<li>In the Content Review view, browse or search for the article you want to set a reminder on</li>
<li>Click <strong>Set Reminder</strong> next to the article</li>
<li>Set a <strong>review date</strong> — the date by which this article should be reviewed</li>
<li>Assign the review to a <strong>team member</strong> (or yourself)</li>
<li>Optionally set a <strong>recurrence</strong> — so after the review is done, the next reminder is automatically created (e.g., every 3 months, every 6 months, or annually)</li>
<li>Click <strong>Save</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>What Happens on the Review Date</h2>
<p>When the review date arrives, the assigned team member receives:</p>
<ul>
<li>A <strong>notification</strong> in FinalDoc (bell icon)</li>
<li>An <strong>email notification</strong> (if email notifications are enabled)</li>
</ul>
<p>The notification links directly to the article so the reviewer can open and check it immediately.</p>

<h2>Completing a Review</h2>
<p>After reviewing an article (and updating it if needed):</p>
<ol>
<li>Go back to <strong>Toolbox → Content Review</strong></li>
<li>Find the article in the pending reviews list</li>
<li>Click <strong>Mark as Reviewed</strong></li>
<li>If a recurrence was set, the next review reminder is automatically created for the future date</li>
</ol>

<h2>Reviewing the Review Dashboard</h2>
<p>The Content Review tool shows a dashboard with:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Overdue</strong> — Articles whose review date has passed but are not yet marked as reviewed</li>
<li><strong>Due soon</strong> — Articles due for review in the next 7–30 days</li>
<li><strong>All upcoming</strong> — Complete list of scheduled reviews sorted by date</li>
</ul>

<h2>Best Practices</h2>
<ul>
<li>Set review reminders on all articles when you first publish them — a 6-month or 12-month cycle works well for most documentation</li>
<li>For rapidly changing content (release notes, pricing), use shorter cycles like 1–3 months</li>
<li>For stable reference content (glossary, compliance docs), an annual review is usually sufficient</li>
<li>Assign reviews to the team member who owns that topic — they are best positioned to know what has changed</li>
</ul>
