<h1>Activity Heatmap &amp; Contribution Tracking</h1>
<p>The Activity Heatmap shows a visual grid of your team's documentation activity over the past year — similar to GitHub's contribution graph. Each square represents one day; the darker the square, the more documentation activity happened that day. Use it to spot productivity patterns, active streaks, and quiet periods.</p>

<h2>Opening the Activity Heatmap</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Activity Heatmap &amp; Contribution Tracking</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Reading the Heatmap</h2>
<p>The heatmap shows one square per day, organized by week (columns) and day of week (rows). Colors:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Gray/empty</strong> — No activity that day</li>
<li><strong>Light green</strong> — Low activity (1–3 actions)</li>
<li><strong>Medium green</strong> — Moderate activity (4–10 actions)</li>
<li><strong>Dark green</strong> — High activity (11+ actions)</li>
</ul>
<p>Hover over any square to see the exact date and count of actions for that day.</p>

<h2>What Counts as Activity</h2>
<p>The heatmap counts the following actions:</p>
<ul>
<li>Articles created</li>
<li>Articles saved/updated</li>
<li>Articles published</li>
<li>Articles moved or reorganized</li>
<li>Comments posted</li>
</ul>
<p>Reader activity (article views, searches) is NOT counted — this is team/author activity only.</p>

<h2>Filtering by Team Member</h2>
<p>By default, the heatmap shows activity from all team members combined. To see an individual's activity:</p>
<ol>
<li>Use the <strong>Team Member</strong> dropdown above the heatmap</li>
<li>Select a specific team member</li>
<li>The heatmap updates to show only their contributions</li>
</ol>

<h2>Contribution Timeline</h2>
<p>Below the heatmap, the <strong>Contribution Timeline</strong> shows a chronological list of recent activity — who created or updated which article and when. This gives context to the heatmap's daily counts.</p>

<h2>Using the Heatmap for Team Management</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Identify active contributors</strong> — Who is doing the most documentation work?</li>
<li><strong>Spot documentation droughts</strong> — Weeks with no activity may mean your KB is falling behind product changes</li>
<li><strong>Celebrate streaks</strong> — Recognize team members with long active streaks</li>
<li><strong>Plan documentation sprints</strong> — Use gaps in the heatmap to schedule focused documentation time</li>
</ul>
