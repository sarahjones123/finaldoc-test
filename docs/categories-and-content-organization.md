<h1>Categories and Content Organization</h1>
<p>Categories are the folders of your knowledge base. They give your articles structure and help readers navigate to what they need. This guide covers everything about creating, organizing, and managing categories.</p>

<h2>What Are Categories?</h2>
<p>A category is a named group that holds related articles. Think of them like chapters in a book, or folders on your desktop. Readers see categories in the left sidebar of your public knowledge base — they expand to reveal the articles inside.</p>
<p>FinalDoc supports <strong>nested categories</strong> — you can create sub-categories inside categories for deeper organization (up to 3 levels deep).</p>

<h2>Creating a Category</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Documentation</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>At the top of the article tree panel (left side), click the <strong>folder + icon</strong> or look for a <strong>New Category</strong> button</li>
<li>Enter a name for the category and press <strong>Enter</strong></li>
</ol>
<p>The new category appears in the tree, ready to receive articles.</p>

<h2>Creating a Sub-Category</h2>
<ol>
<li>Hover over an existing category in the left panel</li>
<li>Click the <strong>+</strong> icon that appears to the right of the category name</li>
<li>Choose <strong>Add Sub-category</strong></li>
<li>Enter the sub-category name and press <strong>Enter</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Renaming a Category</h2>
<ul>
<li>Double-click the category name in the sidebar, type the new name, and press Enter</li>
<li>Or right-click the category → <strong>Rename</strong></li>
</ul>

<h2>Reordering Categories and Articles</h2>
<p>The order of categories and articles in the sidebar is exactly the order readers see them. To change the order:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Drag and drop</strong> — Grab any category or article by its handle (six-dot icon on the left) and drag it to the desired position</li>
<li>Drag an article into a different category to move it</li>
<li>Drag a category above or below another category to reorder them</li>
</ul>
<p>Changes take effect immediately and are visible to readers right away.</p>

<h2>Category Descriptions</h2>
<p>Each category can have a description that appears as a subtitle below the category name in the public KB. To add or edit:</p>
<ol>
<li>Right-click the category → <strong>Edit</strong></li>
<li>Enter the description in the field provided</li>
<li>Save</li>
</ol>

<h2>Category Colors</h2>
<p>You can assign a color to each category — it shows as a colored indicator in the admin sidebar, making it easy to visually identify categories at a glance:</p>
<ol>
<li>Right-click a category → <strong>Change Color</strong></li>
<li>Pick a color from the color palette</li>
<li>The color is applied immediately in the admin view</li>
</ol>

<h2>Moving Articles Between Categories</h2>
<p>To move an article to a different category:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Drag and drop</strong> — Drag the article from one category and drop it into another in the sidebar</li>
<li><strong>Right-click method</strong> — Right-click the article → <strong>Move to</strong> → select destination category</li>
</ul>

<h2>Deleting a Category</h2>
<p>To delete a category:</p>
<ol>
<li>Right-click the category → <strong>Delete</strong></li>
<li>You will be asked to confirm</li>
<li>If the category contains articles, you have two options: <strong>Move articles to another category</strong> or <strong>Delete articles too</strong></li>
</ol>

<div style="border-left: 4px solid #ef4444; border-radius: 8px; padding: 16px; margin: 16px 0; background: rgba(239,68,68,0.08);">
<strong>Caution:</strong> Deleting a category with articles inside and choosing "Delete articles too" is irreversible in a single click. Articles are moved to the Recycle Bin and can be recovered within 30 days.
</div>

<h2>Category Visibility</h2>
<p>Categories inherit the project's site visibility settings. If your project is set to <strong>Mixed Visibility</strong>, individual categories can be marked as private (requires reader login) or public (accessible to everyone). Configure this in <strong>Settings → Security &amp; Access → Content Access &amp; Mixed Visibility</strong>.</p>

<h2>Best Practices for Content Organization</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Keep categories broad</strong> — Too many top-level categories creates navigation clutter. Aim for 5–10 top-level categories</li>
<li><strong>Use descriptive names</strong> — "Getting Started", "API Reference", "Troubleshooting" are clear. "General" or "Misc" are not</li>
<li><strong>Put key articles first</strong> — Drag the most-read articles to the top of each category</li>
<li><strong>Use sub-categories for depth</strong> — If a category has more than 15 articles, consider splitting it with sub-categories</li>
<li><strong>Pin important articles</strong> — Use the pin feature to keep critical articles pinned at the top of their category regardless of order</li>
</ul>
