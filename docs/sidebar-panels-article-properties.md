<h1>Sidebar Panels and Article Properties</h1>
<p>The right side of the Documentation editor contains the <strong>Inspector panel</strong> — a set of panels that control article-level settings and collaboration tools. This guide covers everything available in those panels.</p>

<h2>How to Open and Close the Inspector</h2>
<ul>
<li>Click the <strong>Inspector icon</strong> (usually a sidebar toggle arrow) in the top-right of the editor</li>
<li>Or press <strong>Ctrl+Shift+I / Cmd+Shift+I</strong></li>
<li>The Inspector collapses and expands from the right side of the screen</li>
</ul>

<h2>Article Details Panel</h2>
<p>The Details panel contains the core article properties:</p>

<h3>Title</h3>
<p>The article title is editable both at the top of the editor (the large heading) and in the Details panel. The title is used as the article heading visible to readers, the browser tab title, and the default for the article's slug (URL).</p>

<h3>Description</h3>
<p>A 1–3 sentence summary of the article. This appears in:</p>
<ul>
<li>Search results (both in the admin panel and the public KB search)</li>
<li>The article card when listing articles in a category</li>
<li>Social media previews (Open Graph description) when the article URL is shared</li>
</ul>
<p>Always fill in a description. Articles without descriptions look incomplete in search results.</p>

<h3>Featured Image</h3>
<p>A thumbnail image for the article. Click <strong>Set Featured Image</strong> to upload or choose from Drive. The image is displayed in article listing cards and is used as the social media preview image (Open Graph). Recommended size: 1200 × 630 pixels.</p>

<h3>Tags</h3>
<p>Keywords that categorize the article. Tags help readers find related content and allow you to filter articles in the admin panel. Click the tag field and start typing to add existing tags or create new ones.</p>

<h3>Slug (URL)</h3>
<p>The URL path for this article. Auto-generated from the title (spaces become hyphens, special characters are removed). You can customize it. Changing the slug after publishing creates a broken link if readers have bookmarked the old URL — use redirects (Settings → Domain &amp; SEO → Redirects) to handle this.</p>

<h3>Author</h3>
<p>The displayed author name. Defaults to the currently logged-in user. You can change it to another team member's name. Note: this is the displayed author, not the editing access — any editor can edit any article regardless of author.</p>

<h3>Reading Time</h3>
<p>Estimated reading time displayed at the top of the article on the public KB. Automatically calculated from word count (~200 words per minute). You can override this manually in the Details panel.</p>

<h3>Pinned</h3>
<p>Toggle to pin this article at the top of its category in the public KB sidebar. Pinned articles always appear first, regardless of their normal sort position.</p>

<h3>Change Note</h3>
<p>An optional note you can add before saving. Describes what you changed in this save — stored in Version History so team members can see a changelog at a glance.</p>

<h2>Publishing Panel</h2>
<p>Contains the article's publication status and related controls:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Status badge</strong> — Shows current status: Draft, In Review, Published, Archived</li>
<li><strong>Publish button</strong> — Makes the article live</li>
<li><strong>Set to Draft button</strong> — Unpublishes a live article</li>
<li><strong>Schedule</strong> — Set a future publish date and time</li>
</ul>

<h2>Version History Panel</h2>
<p>Shows all saved versions of the article, newest first. Each version entry shows the date, author name, and change note. Click any version to preview it; click Restore to roll back. See the <strong>Article Versioning and History</strong> article for full details.</p>

<h2>Discuss Tab (Article Comments)</h2>
<p>Shows all article-level comments and replies. Team members leave notes, questions, and feedback here. You can @mention teammates, reply to threads, and resolve completed discussions. See <strong>Comments, Notes, and Collaboration</strong> for full details.</p>

<h2>Inline Tab (Inline Comments)</h2>
<p>Lists all inline comments — comments anchored to specific highlighted text in the article. Shows unresolved inline comments with the highlighted text excerpt and the comment. Click any entry to jump to the highlighted text in the editor.</p>
