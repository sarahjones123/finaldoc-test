<h1>Team Management</h1>
<p>FinalDoc supports multi-user teams where each member has a role that controls what they can see and do. This guide covers how to invite, manage, and remove team members.</p>

<h2>Opening Team Management</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Security &amp; Access</strong> → <strong>Team Management</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Team Roles</h2>
<p>FinalDoc has five roles, from most to least access:</p>
<table>
<thead><tr><th>Role</th><th>What They Can Do</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><strong>Owner</strong></td><td>Everything — manage billing, delete the account, promote/demote admins. Only one Owner per project (typically the account creator)</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Admin</strong></td><td>Full content and settings access. Cannot manage billing or delete the account</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Editor</strong></td><td>Create, edit, and publish articles in any workspace. Cannot change project settings</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Draft Writer</strong></td><td>Create and edit their own articles. Can submit for review but cannot publish directly. Cannot edit other members' articles</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Viewer</strong></td><td>Read-only access to the admin panel and all articles (including drafts). Cannot create or edit anything</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<h2>Inviting a Team Member</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Invite Member</strong></li>
<li>Enter their <strong>email address</strong></li>
<li>Enter their <strong>name</strong> (optional — they can fill this in themselves)</li>
<li>Select their <strong>role</strong></li>
<li>Click <strong>Send Invite</strong></li>
<li>The invitee receives an email invitation with a link to join the team</li>
<li>When they click the link, they set up their account and are added to your project</li>
</ol>
<p>Pending invites appear in the team list as "Invited" until the recipient accepts.</p>

<h2>Changing a Team Member's Role</h2>
<ol>
<li>Find the team member in the list</li>
<li>Click the role badge next to their name</li>
<li>Select the new role from the dropdown</li>
<li>The change takes effect immediately — no confirmation required</li>
</ol>
<p>Only Owners can promote team members to Admin or demote existing Admins.</p>

<h2>Removing a Team Member</h2>
<ol>
<li>Find the team member in the list</li>
<li>Click the <strong>three-dot menu (⋮)</strong> on the right side of their row</li>
<li>Click <strong>Remove from Team</strong></li>
<li>Confirm the removal</li>
</ol>
<p>Removed team members immediately lose access to the admin panel. Their authored articles remain and are not deleted. Any pending approval workflows they were assigned to are not affected.</p>

<h2>Resending an Invite</h2>
<p>If a team member did not receive their invite email:</p>
<ol>
<li>Find the pending invite in the team list (it shows as "Invited")</li>
<li>Click the three-dot menu → <strong>Resend Invite</strong></li>
<li>The invite email is sent again</li>
</ol>
<p>Also check that the invite didn't land in their spam folder. The invite email is sent from <strong>hello@finaldoc.io</strong>.</p>

<h2>Content Access</h2>
<p>Team Management also includes a <strong>Content Access</strong> sub-section where you can control which team members have access to specific workspaces or categories. This is useful when you have multiple knowledge base versions or want to restrict certain editors to specific content areas.</p>
<p>To configure content access:</p>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → Security &amp; Access → Team Management</strong></li>
<li>Click the <strong>Content Access</strong> tab</li>
<li>Select a team member and configure their access to specific workspaces or categories</li>
</ol>
