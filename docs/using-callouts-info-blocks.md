<h1>Using Callouts and Info Blocks</h1>
<p>Callout blocks are coloured, eye-catching boxes you can add inside your articles to highlight important information. They break up the wall of text and make readers stop and pay attention to key points — warnings, tips, notes, and danger alerts.</p>

<h2>Available Callout Types</h2>
<table>
<thead><tr><th>Type</th><th>Color</th><th>When to Use</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><strong>Info</strong></td><td>Blue</td><td>Extra context or general notes that are helpful but not critical. Example: "Note: this feature is only available on the Pro plan."</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Tip</strong></td><td>Green</td><td>Helpful suggestions, shortcuts, or best practices. Example: "Tip: Use keyboard shortcuts to write faster."</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Warning</strong></td><td>Yellow/Amber</td><td>Cautions that could cause unexpected behavior if ignored. Example: "Warning: Changing this setting requires a browser refresh."</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Danger</strong></td><td>Red</td><td>Critical alerts about destructive or irreversible actions. Example: "Danger: Deleting an account removes all data permanently."</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<h2>How to Insert a Callout Block</h2>
<ol>
<li>Place your cursor on an empty line where you want the callout to appear</li>
<li>Click the <strong>Callout</strong> button in the editor toolbar (it looks like a speech bubble or exclamation mark)</li>
<li>Select the callout type from the dropdown: Info, Tip, Warning, or Danger</li>
<li>The callout block appears. Click inside it and start typing your message</li>
</ol>

<h2>Formatting Inside Callouts</h2>
<p>You can use most formatting options inside a callout block:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bold, italic, and inline code</li>
<li>Links (click here, press Ctrl+K)</li>
<li>Bullet lists and numbered lists</li>
</ul>
<p>Keep callout content concise. A callout that is too long loses its impact — if you need more than a few sentences, consider restructuring the article instead.</p>

<h2>Changing the Callout Type</h2>
<p>To change a callout from one type to another after you have created it:</p>
<ol>
<li>Click anywhere inside the callout block</li>
<li>Look for the type selector that appears in the block's header area</li>
<li>Click it and select a different type</li>
<li>The color and icon update immediately</li>
</ol>

<h2>Deleting a Callout Block</h2>
<ul>
<li>Click the callout to select it</li>
<li>Press <strong>Backspace</strong> or <strong>Delete</strong> twice (once to clear the content, once to remove the block itself)</li>
<li>Or right-click the callout → <strong>Delete Block</strong></li>
</ul>

<h2>Best Practices</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Use sparingly</strong> — If every section has a callout, readers stop paying attention to them. Reserve them for truly important points</li>
<li><strong>Match the type to the severity</strong> — Do not use Danger for minor notes. It reduces the impact of real danger warnings</li>
<li><strong>Keep it short</strong> — One or two sentences is ideal. Three sentences max</li>
<li><strong>Put callouts after the relevant content</strong> — Do not open an article with a callout. Explain the concept first, then add the callout as a follow-up</li>
</ul>
