<h1>One-Click AI Translation</h1>
<p>FinalDoc can translate your articles into other languages automatically using AI — without leaving the editor. Translated versions are stored as separate articles, keeping your original content untouched.</p>

<h2>How It Works</h2>
<p>When you translate an article:</p>
<ol>
<li>FinalDoc sends the article title, description, and content to the AI translation engine</li>
<li>The AI translates all three sections in parallel into your chosen language</li>
<li>A new article is created in the same workspace with the translated content</li>
<li>The translated article is saved as a Draft — you review and publish it when ready</li>
</ol>

<h2>How to Translate an Article</h2>
<ol>
<li>Open the article you want to translate in the Documentation editor</li>
<li>In the top toolbar, look for the <strong>Translate</strong> button (globe icon)</li>
<li>Click it to open the translation dropdown</li>
<li>Select the target language from the list</li>
<li>Click <strong>Translate</strong></li>
<li>Wait a few seconds while the AI translates the content</li>
<li>A new article is created with the translated content. A link or notification appears when it is ready</li>
</ol>

<h2>Supported Languages</h2>
<p>FinalDoc supports translation to and from all major world languages, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese</li>
<li>Chinese (Simplified and Traditional), Japanese, Korean</li>
<li>Arabic, Hindi, Russian, Turkish</li>
<li>Dutch, Polish, Swedish, Norwegian, Danish, Finnish</li>
<li>And many more (any language supported by the underlying AI model)</li>
</ul>
<p>The translation quality is highest for widely-spoken languages. For less common languages, always have a native speaker review the output before publishing.</p>

<h2>Managing Translated Articles</h2>
<p>Translated articles are regular articles in your workspace. You can:</p>
<ul>
<li>Edit them like any other article to fix translation errors or add localized context</li>
<li>Publish them when they are ready for readers</li>
<li>Add them to a separate category (e.g., "Español" or "French Docs") to organize multi-language content</li>
<li>Use the <strong>Language Selector</strong> on your public KB (if enabled) to let readers switch between language versions of the same article</li>
</ul>

<h2>Language Selector on the Public KB</h2>
<p>If you have published articles in multiple languages, readers can see a language selector dropdown on article pages to switch to available translations. Enable this in <strong>Settings → General → Localization &amp; Multi-language Setup</strong>.</p>

<h2>Limitations</h2>
<ul>
<li>Translation works on text content only — images, code blocks, and diagrams are not translated</li>
<li>Very long articles may be split into multiple translation requests — the final result is reassembled automatically</li>
<li>Technical terms (product names, command syntax, API parameter names) are preserved as-is</li>
<li>Translation quality depends on the AI model — always review outputs before publishing</li>
</ul>

<h2>Re-translating After Updates</h2>
<p>If you update the original article, existing translations become outdated. FinalDoc does not automatically re-translate — you need to:</p>
<ol>
<li>Open the updated original article</li>
<li>Run the translation again for each language</li>
<li>The new translation overwrites or creates a new draft (depending on your settings)</li>
</ol>
