<h1>Content Health Dashboard</h1>
<p>The Content Health Dashboard gives you an overview of your knowledge base's overall documentation quality. It identifies articles that are missing key elements, need updating, or are likely to frustrate readers — so you can prioritize your documentation improvement work.</p>

<h2>Opening Content Health</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Content Health Dashboard</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Health Checks</h2>
<p>The dashboard runs the following checks on all published articles:</p>

<table>
<thead><tr><th>Check</th><th>What It Flags</th><th>Why It Matters</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><strong>No description</strong></td><td>Articles without a meta description</td><td>Descriptions appear in search results and improve SEO. Without them, your articles look incomplete in search</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>No featured image</strong></td><td>Articles without a thumbnail image</td><td>Featured images make article listings visually appealing and improve social sharing</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>No tags</strong></td><td>Articles with zero tags assigned</td><td>Tags improve discoverability and allow readers to find related content</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Stale content</strong></td><td>Articles not updated in 6+ months</td><td>Outdated documentation erodes reader trust. These articles should be reviewed and refreshed</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Low feedback score</strong></td><td>Articles with more thumbs down than thumbs up</td><td>Readers are explicitly telling you these articles are not helpful. Priority improvement targets</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Short content</strong></td><td>Articles with very few words (under 100)</td><td>Very short articles often lack enough detail to be genuinely useful</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Broken links</strong></td><td>Articles containing links to unavailable URLs</td><td>Broken links frustrate readers and hurt SEO</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<h2>Summary View</h2>
<p>The top of the dashboard shows summary counts:</p>
<ul>
<li>Total published articles</li>
<li>Articles needing attention (with any health issue)</li>
<li>Overall health score (percentage of articles with no health issues)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Fixing Issues</h2>
<p>Click any health check category to see the list of articles that failed that check. Click any article in the list to open it for editing. Fix the issue (add a description, update the content, etc.) and the article will be removed from that health check on the next analysis run.</p>

<h2>Running a Fresh Analysis</h2>
<p>Click <strong>Refresh Analysis</strong> to re-run all health checks against your current published articles. Do this after making improvements to see updated scores.</p>
