<h1>Comments, Notes, and Collaboration</h1>
<p>FinalDoc supports two types of comments for team collaboration on articles: <strong>Article Comments</strong> (a threaded discussion attached to the whole article) and <strong>Inline Comments</strong> (comments anchored to specific highlighted text). Both help your team review, discuss, and improve content without cluttering the article itself.</p>

<h2>Article Comments (Discuss Tab)</h2>
<p>Article-level comments appear in the right-side panel under the <strong>Discuss</strong> tab. They are general conversations about the article as a whole — questions, suggestions, approvals, or review notes.</p>

<h3>How to Leave an Article Comment</h3>
<ol>
<li>Open an article in the editor</li>
<li>Click the <strong>Discuss</strong> tab in the right Inspector panel (you may see a speech bubble icon)</li>
<li>Type your comment in the text box at the bottom</li>
<li>Press <strong>Enter</strong> or click <strong>Post</strong></li>
</ol>

<h3>Replying to a Comment</h3>
<p>Click <strong>Reply</strong> underneath any comment to start a threaded conversation. Replies are nested under the parent comment, keeping discussions organized.</p>

<h3>Mentioning Teammates</h3>
<p>Type <strong>@</strong> followed by a teammate's name to mention them. They will receive a notification and can click the notification to jump directly to the comment. Use @mentions to request specific people to review, approve, or provide input.</p>

<h3>Resolving Comments</h3>
<p>Once a discussion is complete, click <strong>Resolve</strong> on the comment. Resolved comments are hidden by default but can be shown again by toggling "Show Resolved" at the top of the Discuss panel.</p>

<h3>Editing and Deleting Your Comments</h3>
<ul>
<li>Hover over your own comment and click the <strong>pencil icon</strong> to edit it</li>
<li>Click the <strong>trash icon</strong> to delete your comment</li>
<li>Admins can delete any team member's comments</li>
</ul>

<h2>Inline Comments</h2>
<p>Inline comments are anchored to a specific piece of text inside the article. They are perfect for pointing out a specific sentence that needs revision, asking about a particular detail, or flagging a factual error.</p>

<h3>How to Create an Inline Comment</h3>
<ol>
<li>Select (highlight) the text you want to comment on</li>
<li>A small comment bubble icon appears near the selection</li>
<li>Click it — or right-click and choose <strong>Add Comment</strong></li>
<li>Type your comment in the popup that appears</li>
<li>Click <strong>Post</strong></li>
</ol>
<p>The commented text is highlighted in yellow in the editor. Other team members see the yellow highlight and can click it to read and reply to the comment.</p>

<h3>Viewing Inline Comments</h3>
<ul>
<li>Click any yellow highlighted text in the editor to open the comment thread for that selection</li>
<li>Or open the <strong>Inline</strong> tab in the right Inspector panel to see all inline comments for the article in a list</li>
<li>The badge number on the Inline tab shows how many unresolved inline comments the article has</li>
</ul>

<h3>Resolving Inline Comments</h3>
<p>Once an inline comment has been addressed (you fixed the text or answered the question):</p>
<ol>
<li>Click the highlighted text to open the comment</li>
<li>Click <strong>Resolve</strong></li>
<li>The yellow highlight disappears from the article text</li>
<li>The resolved comment can still be viewed in the Inline tab under "Show Resolved"</li>
</ol>

<h2>Notifications</h2>
<p>FinalDoc sends notifications when:</p>
<ul>
<li>Someone comments on an article you authored or are watching</li>
<li>Someone replies to your comment</li>
<li>Someone @mentions you in a comment</li>
<li>An inline comment on your article is resolved</li>
</ul>
<p>Notifications appear in the bell icon in the top navigation bar. You can also configure email or Slack notifications in <strong>Settings → General → Notifications</strong>.</p>

<h2>Collaboration Best Practices</h2>
<ul>
<li>Use <strong>inline comments</strong> for specific edits ("This sentence is confusing — can we rephrase?") and <strong>article comments</strong> for broader discussions ("Is this article ready to publish?")</li>
<li>Always <strong>resolve comments</strong> after addressing them so the Discuss tab stays clean</li>
<li>Use <strong>@mentions</strong> to assign responsibility — otherwise comments can sit unanswered for days</li>
<li>Combine comments with the <strong>Approval Flow</strong> in Toolbox for formal review processes</li>
</ul>
