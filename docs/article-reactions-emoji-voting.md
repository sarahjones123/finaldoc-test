<h1>Article Reactions and Emoji Voting</h1>
<p>Article Reactions let readers express how they feel about your articles beyond a simple thumbs up/down. Readers can react with one of five emoji — giving you richer signal about how your content resonates emotionally. This is separate from the article feedback (thumbs up/down + comment) system.</p>

<h2>Available Reactions</h2>
<p>Readers can choose from five emoji reactions on each article:</p>
<ul>
<li>👍 <strong>Like</strong> — General approval, found it useful</li>
<li>❤️ <strong>Love</strong> — Strong enthusiasm, really helpful or beautifully written</li>
<li>👏 <strong>Clap</strong> — Great explanation, well-written, impressive</li>
<li>🤔 <strong>Thinking</strong> — Interesting but left questions, made them think</li>
<li>🚀 <strong>Rocket</strong> — Advanced content, exciting, innovative</li>
</ul>

<h2>Enabling Article Reactions</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Appearance → Article Display</strong></li>
<li>Find <strong>Show Article Reactions</strong> and toggle it ON</li>
<li>The emoji reaction bar now appears at the bottom of every article on your public knowledge base</li>
</ol>
<p>You can also disable reactions on a specific article via the article's Properties panel (right side of the editor) → Reader Experience → Disable Reactions.</p>

<h2>How Readers Use Reactions</h2>
<ol>
<li>Reader scrolls to the bottom of an article</li>
<li>They see five emoji buttons below the article</li>
<li>They click the emoji that matches how the article made them feel</li>
<li>The emoji briefly animates to confirm the reaction was registered</li>
<li>The count next to that emoji increases by 1</li>
</ol>
<p>Each reader can leave one reaction per article. If they click a different emoji, it replaces their previous reaction (the old count goes down, the new count goes up). Reactions are tracked per session/account — anonymous visitors by session cookie, logged-in readers by account.</p>

<h2>Viewing Reaction Data</h2>

<h3>On the Public KB</h3>
<p>Reaction counts are visible to all readers at the bottom of each article. Readers can see how others have reacted.</p>

<h3>In the Admin Panel</h3>
<p>To see the full reaction breakdown for your articles:</p>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Go to <strong>Article Analytics</strong></li>
<li>Click any article to view its detail page</li>
<li>The reaction breakdown shows how many of each emoji type were received</li>
</ol>
<p>The article list view also shows a summary reaction count alongside views and feedback scores.</p>

<h2>Reactions vs. Feedback</h2>
<table>
<thead><tr><th>Feature</th><th>Article Reactions</th><th>Article Feedback</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td>Input type</td><td>Emoji tap (1 click)</td><td>Thumbs up/down + optional comment</td></tr>
<tr><td>Effort required</td><td>Minimal — 1 click</td><td>Low to moderate — click + optional text</td></tr>
<tr><td>What it tells you</td><td>Emotional response to the content</td><td>Whether the article solved the reader's problem</td></tr>
<tr><td>Response rate</td><td>Higher (very easy)</td><td>Lower (requires decision)</td></tr>
<tr><td>Best for</td><td>Content quality signal</td><td>Support effectiveness signal</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>Both can be enabled at the same time — they serve different purposes and complement each other.</p>

<h2>Using Reactions to Improve Content</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>High 👍 + ❤️</strong> — Your content is resonating well. Consider making more articles like this</li>
<li><strong>High 🤔</strong> — The content raised questions or wasn't fully clear. Consider adding a FAQ section or clarifying complex parts</li>
<li><strong>High 👏</strong> — Excellent content that readers appreciate. Good candidate for featuring on the homepage</li>
<li><strong>Low reactions overall</strong> — Readers may not be scrolling to the bottom, or the article may need to be shorter and more focused</li>
</ul>
