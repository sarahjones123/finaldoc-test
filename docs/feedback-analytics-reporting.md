<h1>Feedback Analytics &amp; Reporting</h1>
<p>Beyond the individual feedback inbox, FinalDoc provides aggregate analytics on reader feedback trends — satisfaction scores over time, feedback volume, and which articles are getting the most negative feedback. Use this to measure the overall health of your knowledge base and track improvement over time.</p>

<h2>Opening Feedback Analytics</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Feedback Analytics</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Overall Satisfaction Score</h2>
<p>The top of the page shows your KB's overall satisfaction score:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Satisfaction rate</strong> — Percentage of feedback submissions that were positive (thumbs up)</li>
<li><strong>Total feedback received</strong> — All ratings in the selected period</li>
<li><strong>Positive vs. Negative</strong> — Absolute counts</li>
</ul>
<p>Industry benchmarks: A knowledge base satisfaction rate above 70% is considered good; above 85% is excellent.</p>

<h2>Satisfaction Trend Chart</h2>
<p>A line chart shows weekly satisfaction rate over time. Use this to measure the impact of documentation improvements:</p>
<ul>
<li>Made major updates to your top articles in January? Did the satisfaction rate improve in February?</li>
<li>Released a major product change? Did satisfaction dip as readers adjusted to the new UI?</li>
</ul>

<h2>Bottom-Ranked Articles by Feedback</h2>
<p>A table shows articles sorted by lowest satisfaction rate. These are your documentation problem areas:</p>
<ul>
<li>Article title</li>
<li>Total feedback received</li>
<li>Satisfaction rate (%)</li>
<li>Last updated date</li>
<li>Quick link to open the article for editing</li>
</ul>

<h2>Feedback Volume by Article</h2>
<p>A different view shows articles by total feedback volume (most feedback received). High-volume articles with good satisfaction rates are your star content — study them to understand what makes documentation effective. High-volume articles with bad satisfaction are your biggest problem — they have lots of readers but aren't helping them.</p>

<h2>Date Range Filtering</h2>
<p>All feedback analytics are filterable by date range. Compare periods to measure improvement over time (e.g., "Before redesign" vs. "After redesign").</p>

<h2>Exporting Feedback Data</h2>
<p>Click <strong>Export CSV</strong> to download all individual feedback entries with ratings, comments, article names, and dates. Useful for stakeholder reports or feeding data into external analytics tools.</p>
