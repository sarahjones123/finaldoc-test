<h1>Article Display</h1>
<p>The Article Display settings control what elements appear on individual article pages of your public knowledge base. Toggle features on or off to create the reading experience that works best for your audience.</p>

<h2>Opening Article Display</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Appearance</strong> → <strong>Article Display</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Available Toggles</h2>
<table>
<thead><tr><th>Setting</th><th>What It Controls</th><th>Default</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><strong>Show Author</strong></td><td>Displays the author's name (and avatar if available) below the article title</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Last Updated Date</strong></td><td>Shows "Last updated: [date]" below the title — helpful for readers to know if content is current</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Reading Time</strong></td><td>Shows estimated reading time next to the date (e.g., "5 min read")</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Table of Contents</strong></td><td>Auto-generates and displays a table of contents sidebar on the right for articles with H2/H3 headings</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Feedback</strong></td><td>Shows the "Was this helpful? 👍 👎" section at the bottom of every article</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Article Reactions</strong></td><td>Shows the emoji reaction bar (👍 ❤️ 👏 🤔 🚀) below article content — readers click to react</td><td>Off</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Tags</strong></td><td>Displays article tags as clickable chips below the title</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Related Articles</strong></td><td>Shows AI-suggested related articles at the bottom of each article</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Reading Progress Bar</strong></td><td>Shows a thin progress bar at the top of the browser as the reader scrolls through the article</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Listen Button</strong></td><td>Shows a "Listen" (audio play) button that reads the article aloud using AI text-to-speech</td><td>Off</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Breadcrumbs</strong></td><td>Shows the category → sub-category → article navigation trail above the title</td><td>On</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Print/PDF Button</strong></td><td>Shows a button for readers to download the article as PDF or print it</td><td>Off</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Show Share Button</strong></td><td>Shows a share button (copy link, share to social) on articles</td><td>On</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<h2>Saving Changes</h2>
<p>Click <strong>Save</strong> after changing any setting. Changes apply immediately to your live knowledge base with no caching delay.</p>

<h2>Per-Article Overrides</h2>
<p>Some of these settings can be overridden per-article in the Documentation editor's Inspector panel. For example, you can hide the table of contents for a specific article even if it is enabled globally — useful for very short articles where a TOC would be unnecessary.</p>
