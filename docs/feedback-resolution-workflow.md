<h1>Feedback Resolution Workflow</h1>
<p>This article explains how to handle feedback from start to finish &mdash; from receiving reader feedback to resolving it and tracking your team's performance.</p>

<h2>Workflow Overview</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Receive</strong> &mdash; Reader submits feedback (helpful/not helpful) or reports an issue (wrong info, missing step, broken link)</li>
<li><strong>Review</strong> &mdash; Open the Feedback inbox, review the feedback details</li>
<li><strong>Create Task</strong> &mdash; Click "Create Task" to add it to the Home page Tasks card</li>
<li><strong>Assign</strong> &mdash; Go to Home &rarr; Tasks (feedback tasks have IDs like <strong>F-001</strong>), open the task, assign it to the appropriate writer</li>
<li><strong>Fix</strong> &mdash; Writer opens the article (via "Edit Article" button) and makes improvements</li>
<li><strong>Resolve</strong> &mdash; Mark the feedback as resolved with an optional note</li>
</ol>

<h2>Feedback Statuses</h2>
<table>
<thead><tr><th>Status</th><th>Meaning</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><strong>New</strong></td><td>Just received, not yet reviewed</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>In Review</strong></td><td>Being evaluated by the team</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Assigned</strong></td><td>A task has been created and assigned</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Resolved</strong></td><td>The issue has been addressed</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Archived</strong></td><td>Closed without action (e.g., spam or duplicate)</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<h2>Portal Issue Statuses</h2>
<p>Portal Issues (Wrong Info, Missing Step, Broken Link) have their own status workflow:</p>
<table>
<thead><tr><th>Status</th><th>Meaning</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><strong>New</strong></td><td>Just reported</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>In Progress</strong></td><td>Being worked on</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Resolved</strong></td><td>Issue fixed</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Dismissed</strong></td><td>Not an actual issue</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>Change the status by clicking the status badge (top-right corner of each issue card) and selecting a new status.</p>

<h2>SLA Tracking</h2>
<p>FinalDoc tracks a 24-hour response SLA for feedback. Each feedback card shows an SLA indicator:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Green (On track)</strong> &mdash; Within 24 hours of submission</li>
<li><strong>Amber (At risk)</strong> &mdash; Approaching the 24-hour deadline</li>
<li><strong>Red (Breached)</strong> &mdash; More than 24 hours without resolution</li>
</ul>

<h2>Replying to Readers</h2>
<p>You can email a reply to readers who submitted feedback while logged in (readers with an email address). Anonymous readers cannot be replied to.</p>
<ol>
<li>Click the <strong>Reply</strong> (message) icon on a feedback card</li>
<li>Type your response</li>
<li>Click <strong>Send Reply</strong></li>
<li>The reader receives a branded email with your response</li>
</ol>
<p>The Reply button only appears when the reader&rsquo;s email is available.</p>

<h2>Performance Tracking</h2>
<p>The <strong>Your Performance</strong> section in the Article Feedback tab tracks:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Day Streak</strong> &mdash; Consecutive days with 100% feedback resolved</li>
<li><strong>Resolved Count</strong> &mdash; Total feedback items you&rsquo;ve handled</li>
<li><strong>Avg Response Time</strong> &mdash; Days to resolve (with decimals)</li>
<li><strong>Achievements</strong> &mdash; 6 unlockable badges: First Step, Getting Started, Dedicated, Feedback Champion, Week Warrior, Monthly Master</li>
</ul>
