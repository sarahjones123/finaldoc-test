<h1>Reusable Snippets</h1>
<p>Snippets are reusable content blocks that you create once and insert into multiple articles. When you update a snippet, the change appears everywhere it is used — automatically. Use snippets for any content that appears across many articles and needs to stay consistent.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for Snippets</h2>
<ul>
<li>A standard support contact block: "Need help? Email us at support@company.com or chat with us at app.company.com/chat"</li>
<li>A legal disclaimer that appears in every policy-related article</li>
<li>A "prerequisites" block: "Before you begin, make sure you have [X, Y, Z] installed"</li>
<li>A promotional notice: "Upgrade to Pro to access this feature"</li>
<li>A footer with links to related resources that appears on all articles in a category</li>
</ul>

<h2>Creating a Snippet</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Toolbox</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Under <strong>Content Blocks</strong>, click <strong>Reusable Snippets</strong></li>
<li>Click <strong>+ New Snippet</strong></li>
<li>Give the snippet a <strong>name</strong> (internal, not visible to readers) — something descriptive like "Support Contact Block" or "Pro Plan Notice"</li>
<li>Write the snippet content in the editor — you can use all formatting options: headings, lists, links, code blocks, etc.</li>
<li>Click <strong>Save</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Inserting a Snippet into an Article</h2>
<ol>
<li>Open an article in the Documentation editor</li>
<li>Place your cursor where you want the snippet content to appear</li>
<li>Click the <strong>Insert Snippet</strong> button in the editor toolbar (it looks like two overlapping rectangles or a "merge" icon)</li>
<li>A dropdown or dialog shows all your saved snippets</li>
<li>Click the snippet you want to insert</li>
<li>The snippet content is inserted at the cursor position</li>
</ol>

<h2>Updating a Snippet</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go back to <strong>Toolbox → Reusable Snippets</strong></li>
<li>Find the snippet you want to update and click it to edit</li>
<li>Make your changes and click <strong>Save</strong></li>
<li>The updated content automatically appears in all articles that use this snippet — no need to edit each article individually</li>
</ol>

<div style="border-left: 4px solid #3b82f6; border-radius: 8px; padding: 16px; margin: 16px 0; background: rgba(59,130,246,0.08);">
<strong>Note:</strong> Snippets are embedded by reference. The article stores a reference to the snippet, not a copy of the content. This is why updates propagate automatically.
</div>

<h2>Deleting a Snippet</h2>
<p>Deleting a snippet removes it from the Snippets library and also removes all instances where it was inserted in articles (the space where the snippet was is left empty). Make sure you want to remove all instances before deleting.</p>
