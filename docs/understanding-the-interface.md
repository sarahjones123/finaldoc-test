<h1>Understanding the Interface</h1>
<p>FinalDoc's admin interface is built around a consistent layout: a left sidebar for navigation, a top bar for quick actions, and a main content area that changes based on what you're doing. This article explains each area so you can navigate confidently.</p>

<h2>The Left Sidebar</h2>
<p>The sidebar is always visible and gives you access to every section of FinalDoc:</p>

<h3>Core Navigation (top section)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dashboard</strong> — Home page with activity feed, metrics, heatmap, and contribution charts</li>
<li><strong>Documentation</strong> — The article editor. This is where you write, edit, and organize all your content</li>
<li><strong>API Docs</strong> — Upload OpenAPI specifications and generate an interactive API reference for your developers</li>
<li><strong>Toolbox</strong> — Power tools: Find &amp; Replace, Approval Flow, Review Reminders, SEO Metadata, Variables, Snippets, Glossary, Import/Export, PDF Export, AI Optimizer, and Video Studio</li>
<li><strong>External Widget</strong> — Configure the embeddable help widget for your website</li>
<li><strong>Drive</strong> — Upload and manage images, PDFs, and other files</li>
</ul>

<h3>Analytics &amp; Feedback</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Analytics</strong> — Article views, reader sessions, search queries, content health, team performance</li>
<li><strong>Feedback</strong> — Reader ratings, comments, AI chatbot query logs</li>
<li><strong>Ticket Analysis</strong> — AI analysis of support tickets to find documentation gaps</li>
</ul>

<h3>Bottom section</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>AI Agent (AI Agent (OpenClaw))</strong> — Provision and manage the AI bot for WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack, Discord, Teams, and WebChat</li>
<li><strong>Settings</strong> (gear icon) — All project settings: branding, integrations, security, billing, team management</li>
</ul>

<h2>The Top Bar</h2>
<p>The top bar provides quick actions regardless of which section you're in:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>+ Create</strong> — Creates a new article in the current context (or shows a quick picker)</li>
<li><strong>Search</strong> — Full-text search across all articles in the project</li>
<li><strong>View Site</strong> — Opens your public knowledge base in a new browser tab</li>
<li><strong>Plan badge</strong> — Your current subscription plan</li>
<li><strong>Notifications bell</strong> — New feedback, @mentions, approval requests, and system alerts</li>
<li><strong>Profile avatar</strong> — Account settings, notification preferences, and sign out</li>
</ul>

<h2>The Documentation Editor (Three-Panel Layout)</h2>
<p>The Documentation section uses a three-panel layout:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Left panel</strong> — Category tree with all articles nested under their categories. Drag items to reorder. Right-click for options. Has workspace switcher at the top if you have multiple workspaces.</li>
<li><strong>Centre panel (editor)</strong> — The article writing area. Click anywhere to start typing. The toolbar along the top provides all formatting and insertion tools.</li>
<li><strong>Right panel (Inspector)</strong> — Article properties: publish status, SEO description, featured image, tags, author, scheduled publish date, reactions toggle, and more. Toggle it with the Inspector button in the toolbar or <kbd>Ctrl+Shift+I</kbd>.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The Toolbox (Four-Section Layout)</h2>
<p>The Toolbox has a left navigation panel listing all tools organized into four sections: Content, Content Blocks, Import and Export, and AI Tools. Click any tool in the left list to open it in the main area.</p>

<h2>Settings Layout</h2>
<p>Settings has a left menu with all settings sections (General, Appearance, Domain &amp; SEO, Security, Team, AI, Integrations, Data &amp; Compliance, Billing). Click a section to expand it and see sub-pages. A search bar at the top of Settings lets you find any setting by keyword — just type what you're looking for.</p>

<h2>Keyboard Navigation Tips</h2>
<ul>
<li>Press <kbd>Escape</kbd> anywhere in the app to activate the Settings search bar</li>
<li>Press <kbd>?</kbd> while editing an article to see all keyboard shortcuts</li>
<li>Use <kbd>Ctrl+S</kbd> / <kbd>Cmd+S</kbd> to save an article without clicking</li>
<li>Use <kbd>Tab</kbd> to indent list items in the editor</li>
</ul>
