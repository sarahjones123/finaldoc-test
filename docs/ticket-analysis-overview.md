<h1>Ticket Analysis Overview</h1>
<p>Ticket Analysis is a FinalDoc feature that connects your support inbox to your knowledge base. Your support tickets are a goldmine of data about what your customers struggle with — but reading them all manually is not realistic. FinalDoc's AI reads your tickets and identifies patterns, then tells you exactly which documentation topics would prevent the most support requests.</p>

<h2>Opening Ticket Analysis</h2>
<p>Click <strong>Ticket Analysis</strong> (the brain icon) in the left sidebar.</p>

<h2>How It Works</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Connect</strong> — Link your support platform (Freshdesk, Zendesk, or Freshservice)</li>
<li><strong>Analyze</strong> — FinalDoc's AI reads through your recent tickets, categorizes them by topic, and identifies recurring themes</li>
<li><strong>Review</strong> — See a breakdown of the most common ticket topics with AI recommendations for documentation that would address them</li>
<li><strong>Act</strong> — Create new articles or update existing ones based on the recommendations. Track deflection improvement over time</li>
</ol>

<h2>Connecting a Ticket Source</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Connecting Ticket Sources</strong> or the <strong>+ Add Source</strong> button</li>
<li>Choose your support platform: Freshdesk, Zendesk, or Freshservice</li>
<li>Enter your API credentials:
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Freshdesk</strong>: Your Freshdesk subdomain and API key</li>
    <li><strong>Zendesk</strong>: Your Zendesk subdomain, email, and API token</li>
    <li><strong>Freshservice</strong>: Your Freshservice subdomain and API key</li>
  </ul>
</li>
<li>Click <strong>Connect</strong></li>
<li>FinalDoc fetches recent tickets to test the connection</li>
</ol>

<h2>Running an Analysis</h2>
<ol>
<li>After connecting, click <strong>Run Analysis</strong></li>
<li>Choose the date range for tickets to analyze (last 30 days, 90 days, etc.)</li>
<li>Click <strong>Start Analysis</strong></li>
<li>The AI processes the tickets — this may take a few minutes depending on volume</li>
<li>Results appear when complete</li>
</ol>

<h2>Understanding the Results</h2>
<p>The analysis shows:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Top ticket categories</strong> — The most common support topics (e.g., "Password Reset", "Installation Issues", "Billing Questions")</li>
<li><strong>Ticket volume per category</strong> — How many tickets fall into each topic</li>
<li><strong>Existing articles</strong> — Which of your current articles already address each topic</li>
<li><strong>Gaps</strong> — Topics that appear in many tickets but have little or no documentation coverage</li>
<li><strong>Recommendations</strong> — Specific article titles and content ideas the AI suggests creating</li>
</ul>

<h2>Taking Action</h2>
<p>For each recommendation:</p>
<ul>
<li>Click <strong>Generate Draft</strong> — AI writes a draft article on the recommended topic</li>
<li>Click <strong>View Tickets</strong> — See a sample of the actual tickets driving this recommendation for context</li>
<li>Click <strong>Mark as Done</strong> — If you have already addressed this topic, mark it to track progress</li>
</ul>

<h2>Deflection Metrics</h2>
<p>After adding documentation based on recommendations, re-run the analysis in future months. Compare ticket volumes over time to see if new documentation is reducing support load. The Ticket Deflection &amp; Impact Metrics report shows this trend.</p>
