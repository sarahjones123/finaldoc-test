<h1>Toolbox Overview</h1>
<p>The <strong>Toolbox</strong> is your power tools section in FinalDoc. It is where you go for bulk operations, content management tools, AI-powered optimizations, and advanced features that work across your entire knowledge base — not just a single article. Access it by clicking <strong>Toolbox</strong> (the wrench icon) in the left sidebar.</p>

<h2>What Is in the Toolbox?</h2>
<p>The Toolbox is organized into four sections:</p>

<h3>Content</h3>
<p>Tools for managing content across all articles:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Find &amp; Replace</strong> — Search for any word or phrase across all articles and replace it in bulk. Perfect for updating product names, fixing recurring typos, or updating outdated terminology across hundreds of articles at once</li>
<li><strong>Approval Flow</strong> — Design multi-step content approval workflows. Set up review stages with assigned reviewers so articles go through quality checks before publishing</li>
<li><strong>Content Review</strong> — Set reminder schedules for articles so your team gets notified when content is due for review and might be outdated</li>
<li><strong>Content Tags</strong> — Manage all your tags in one place. Create, rename, merge, and delete tags. See which articles use each tag</li>
<li><strong>SEO Metadata</strong> — View and edit SEO descriptions for all articles in a spreadsheet-like view. Much faster than opening each article one by one</li>
</ul>

<h3>Content Blocks</h3>
<p>Reusable content building blocks you can use across articles:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Page Templates</strong> — Create article templates with pre-filled structure. New articles can be created from templates so writers start with the right format instead of a blank page</li>
<li><strong>Dynamic Fields</strong> — Create reusable variables (like your product name, version number, or pricing) that you insert into articles. When you update the variable, it updates everywhere it is used automatically</li>
<li><strong>Reusable Snippets</strong> — Save frequently used content blocks (a standard warning notice, a legal disclaimer, a contact block) and insert them into multiple articles. Editing the snippet updates it everywhere</li>
<li><strong>Term Glossary</strong> — Define your terminology. Terms in the glossary are automatically highlighted in your articles, and readers can hover to see the definition. Also generates a public Glossary page</li>
</ul>

<h3>Import and Export</h3>
<p>Move content in and out of FinalDoc:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Import &amp; Export</strong> — Import articles from Markdown, HTML, Confluence, Zendesk, DOCX, or DITA formats. Export your articles to Markdown, HTML, or JSON</li>
<li><strong>Project Transfer</strong> — Export your entire project as a backup or to migrate to a different FinalDoc account</li>
<li><strong>PDF Export</strong> — Generate branded PDF versions of your articles, categories, or entire knowledge base. Customizable templates with your logo and colors</li>
<li><strong>Content Migration</strong> — Bulk import content from other documentation platforms</li>
<li><strong>Portal Pages</strong> — Create custom static pages that appear alongside your knowledge base (e.g., Terms of Service, About Us)</li>
</ul>

<h3>AI Tools</h3>
<p>AI-powered features to improve and create content:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>AI Optimizer</strong> — Runs an AI health check on all your articles. Identifies articles that score poorly on readability, completeness, SEO, and consistency. Click any article to see specific improvement suggestions</li>
<li><strong>Video Studio (now in AI Studio sidebar)</strong> — Automatically generates professional documentation videos from your articles. The AI writes the script, generates voiceover, captures app screenshots, and assembles a polished video with transitions and subtitles</li>
</ul>

<h2>Getting to Specific Tools</h2>
<p>Click <strong>Toolbox</strong> in the left sidebar to open the Toolbox view. The left side of the Toolbox shows all tools organized by section. Click any tool name to open it in the main area. Your last selected tool is remembered between sessions.</p>
