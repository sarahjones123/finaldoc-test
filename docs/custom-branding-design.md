<h1>Custom Branding and Design</h1>
<p>FinalDoc gives you full control over the look and feel of your public knowledge base. You can apply your brand colors, upload your logo, choose fonts, and customize the layout — all without writing code. Changes preview in real time before you save.</p>

<h2>Where to Customize</h2>
<p>All appearance settings are in <strong>Settings → Appearance</strong>, which has several sub-sections:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Portal Builder</strong> — Main layout, header, footer, homepage sections, and navigation</li>
<li><strong>Design Studio</strong> — Colors, fonts, and visual theme</li>
<li><strong>Article Display</strong> — How individual articles are laid out and what elements show</li>
<li><strong>Labels &amp; Text</strong> — Customize button labels, placeholder text, and UI copy</li>
<li><strong>Smart Bars</strong> — Announcement banners at the top of your KB</li>
</ul>

<h2>Design Studio — Colors and Fonts</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → Appearance → Design Studio</strong></li>
<li>Click the color swatches to set your brand colors:
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Primary color</strong> — Used for links, buttons, active states, and accent highlights</li>
    <li><strong>Secondary color</strong> — Used for secondary accents and hover states</li>
    <li><strong>Background color</strong> — The main KB background</li>
    <li><strong>Text color</strong> — The main body text color</li>
    <li><strong>Border color</strong> — Dividers and card borders</li>
  </ul>
</li>
<li>Choose a <strong>font</strong> for headings and body text from the Google Fonts selection</li>
<li>Toggle <strong>Dark Mode</strong> to preview how your colors look in dark mode</li>
<li>Click <strong>Save</strong> to apply changes</li>
</ol>

<h2>Portal Builder — Layout and Logo</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → Appearance → Portal Builder</strong></li>
<li>Upload your <strong>logo</strong> — appears in the KB header. Recommended: PNG with transparent background, at least 200px wide</li>
<li>Set your <strong>favicon</strong> — the small icon in browser tabs and bookmarks</li>
<li>Choose a <strong>layout style</strong>: Sidebar-left (classic), Centered, or Full-width</li>
<li>Configure the <strong>homepage</strong>:
  <ul>
    <li>Show/hide the search bar, featured articles, category cards, or a hero banner</li>
    <li>Add a custom welcome message or tagline</li>
    <li>Add a hero image or background gradient</li>
  </ul>
</li>
<li>Configure the <strong>header</strong>:
  <ul>
    <li>Show/hide navigation links</li>
    <li>Add a link to your main website ("Back to [Company]")</li>
    <li>Enable/disable the language selector</li>
  </ul>
</li>
</ol>

<h2>Article Display Settings</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings → Appearance → Article Display</strong></li>
<li>Configure what appears on article pages:
  <ul>
    <li>Show/hide author name and avatar</li>
    <li>Show/hide last updated date</li>
    <li>Show/hide reading time</li>
    <li>Show/hide table of contents</li>
    <li>Show/hide related articles</li>
    <li>Show/hide feedback (thumbs up/down)</li>
    <li>Show/hide reactions (emoji voting)</li>
    <li>Show/hide the Listen button (AI audio player)</li>
    <li>Show/hide article tags</li>
  </ul>
</li>
</ol>

<h2>Custom CSS</h2>
<p>If you need deeper customization beyond the visual settings, you can inject custom CSS in <strong>Settings → Appearance → Custom CSS &amp; JavaScript</strong>. This lets you override any visual style in the KB, change spacing, add custom fonts, or create unique layouts. See the <strong>Custom CSS &amp; JavaScript</strong> article for details.</p>

<h2>Labels and Text</h2>
<p>You can change any button label or UI text in <strong>Settings → Appearance → Labels &amp; Text</strong>. For example, rename "Articles" to "Guides", change "Was this helpful?" to "Did this solve your problem?", or localize button text without enabling full multi-language support.</p>

<h2>Previewing Changes</h2>
<p>Every change you make in Appearance settings shows a live preview panel on the right side of the screen (or you can open a full-screen preview). This lets you see exactly how the KB will look before saving. Click <strong>Save</strong> only when you are happy with the result.</p>
