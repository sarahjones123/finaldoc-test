<h1>Portal Builder</h1>
<p>The Portal Builder is your knowledge base's visual design and layout tool. It controls everything your readers see on your public KB — the homepage, header, footer, article pages, login page, error pages, and more. Changes are previewed live in a built-in preview panel as you work, and published to your KB when you click Save.</p>

<h2>Opening Portal Builder</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Settings</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Appearance → Portal Builder</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>KB Mode: Landing Page vs. Doc Hub</h2>
<p>At the very top of the Portal Builder, you choose your KB's homepage mode:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Landing Page</strong> — A rich, customizable homepage with hero banners, feature sections, category cards, and promotional content. Best for external-facing KBs where first impressions matter</li>
<li><strong>Doc Hub</strong> — A simpler, documentation-first homepage that puts the search bar and category list front and center. Best for internal/developer KBs where users want to find info quickly without marketing content</li>
</ul>
<p>Switch between modes at any time. Each mode has its own set of customizable sections.</p>

<h2>Homepage Sections (Landing Page Mode)</h2>
<p>These sections can be reordered (drag-and-drop) and toggled on/off:</p>

<h3>Position / Layout</h3>
<p>Set the overall content width and side padding of your KB homepage.</p>

<h3>Hero / Welcome Area</h3>
<ul>
<li>Title — Main heading (e.g., "Welcome to Our Help Center")</li>
<li>Subtitle — Supporting text under the heading</li>
<li>Search bar — Show/hide the search bar in the hero (strongly recommended: ON)</li>
<li>Background — Solid color, gradient, or background image</li>
</ul>

<h3>Promotional Banner</h3>
<p>An optional promotional strip for announcements or CTAs — can include a headline, subtext, and a link button.</p>

<h3>Homepage Buttons</h3>
<p>Quick-action buttons on the homepage — typically linking to the most important sections or to external resources.</p>

<h3>Search Settings</h3>
<p>Configure search bar behavior — placeholder text, search scope, and whether to show recent searches.</p>

<h3>Category Cards</h3>
<p>Show a grid of category cards on the homepage. Each card displays the category name, icon, and article count. Readers click to browse that category.</p>

<h3>Featured Articles</h3>
<p>Highlight specific articles on the homepage. Either manually pick articles or let FinalDoc auto-select the most-viewed ones.</p>

<h3>Popular Articles</h3>
<p>An auto-populated list of your most-viewed articles. No manual configuration — FinalDoc generates this from view analytics.</p>

<h2>Header Configuration</h2>
<p>The header appears on every page of your KB:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Logo</strong> — Upload your company logo (PNG/SVG, transparent background recommended)</li>
<li><strong>Logo link</strong> — Where clicking the logo navigates to (KB homepage or your main website)</li>
<li><strong>Navigation links</strong> — Add links to other pages in the header nav (external URLs or internal KB categories)</li>
<li><strong>"Back to [site]" link</strong> — A persistent link back to your main website</li>
<li><strong>Language selector</strong> — Show/hide the language dropdown (useful if you have multi-language content)</li>
<li><strong>Dark mode toggle</strong> — Show/hide the theme toggle button so readers can switch between light and dark mode</li>
<li><strong>Reader login button</strong> — Show/hide if reader authentication is enabled</li>
</ul>

<h2>Footer Configuration</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Footer text</strong> — Copyright notice, tagline, or any text</li>
<li><strong>Footer links</strong> — Links to legal pages, social media, or contact pages</li>
<li><strong>Show/hide footer</strong> — Toggle the footer entirely</li>
</ul>

<h2>Article Footer</h2>
<p>Configure what appears below every article on your public KB:</p>
<ul>
<li>Was this article helpful? (thumbs up/down rating)</li>
<li>Article feedback text</li>
<li>Article reactions (emoji voting)</li>
<li>Related articles section</li>
<li>Contact/escalation CTA ("Still need help? Contact us")</li>
</ul>

<h2>Documentation Page Settings</h2>
<p>Control the layout of individual article pages:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Table of Contents</strong> — Show/hide the TOC sidebar that auto-generates from article headings</li>
<li><strong>Reading time</strong> — Show/hide estimated reading time above articles</li>
<li><strong>Author byline</strong> — Show/hide the article author name</li>
<li><strong>Last updated date</strong> — Show/hide the article's last modified date</li>
<li><strong>Breadcrumbs</strong> — Show/hide the category path navigation above articles</li>
<li><strong>Listen button (Audio Player)</strong> — Enable readers to hear articles read aloud via AI text-to-speech</li>
<li><strong>Print button</strong> — Show/hide a print this article button</li>
</ul>

<h2>Login Page</h2>
<p>When reader authentication is enabled, customize how the login page looks:</p>
<ul>
<li>Welcome message and subtitle</li>
<li>Login page logo (can be different from the main KB logo)</li>
<li>Background color or image</li>
<li>Allow/disallow self-registration</li>
<li>Custom registration fields (collect additional info beyond email)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Error Pages</h2>
<p>Customize the pages readers see when something goes wrong:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>404 Not Found</strong> — Page shown when a URL doesn't exist. Customize heading, message, and suggested articles</li>
<li><strong>Access Denied</strong> — Shown when a reader tries to access mixed-visibility private content without being logged in</li>
<li><strong>Unauthorized</strong> — Shown when a logged-in reader lacks permission to view a specific article</li>
<li><strong>IP Restriction</strong> — Shown when a reader's IP address is blocked by your IP restriction settings</li>
</ul>

<h2>Announcements</h2>
<p>The Announcements section lets you manage a dedicated announcements drawer on your KB. Readers can click "Announcements" in the header to see a list of news updates from your team. Configure:</p>
<ul>
<li>Show/hide the announcements entry point in the header</li>
<li>Announcement list title and intro text</li>
<li>Individual announcements are managed in the Announcements panel</li>
</ul>

<h2>AI Chatbot Button Style</h2>
<p>Choose how the Ved AI chatbot button appears on your KB:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Classic</strong> — A round floating button with a chat icon in the corner</li>
<li><strong>Capsule</strong> — A split pill button with Voice on the left and Chat on the right, with an animated cosmic orb in the center. Clicking Voice starts a voice conversation; clicking Chat opens the text chat</li>
<li><strong>Inline</strong> — Embeds a chat input directly on the KB homepage instead of a floating button</li>
</ul>

<h2>Saving Changes</h2>
<p>All changes are previewed live in the panel on the right side of the Portal Builder. Click <strong>Save</strong> to publish your changes and make them live on your public knowledge base.</p>

<h2>Version History</h2>
<p>The Portal Builder tracks past saved designs. Click the <strong>History</strong> icon to view previous saved versions and restore any earlier design if needed.</p>
