<h1>Team Performance Metrics</h1>
<p>The Team Performance Metrics view gives managers and knowledge base owners a picture of how the documentation team is performing — who is contributing, what the output looks like, and how content quality is trending over time. Use it in team retrospectives, performance reviews, or to identify who needs support.</p>

<h2>Opening Team Performance</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Team Performance Metrics</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>Per-Author Stats</h2>
<p>A table shows each team member's contributions for the selected time period:</p>
<table>
<thead><tr><th>Metric</th><th>What It Shows</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><strong>Articles Created</strong></td><td>How many new articles the author created</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Articles Updated</strong></td><td>How many existing articles the author saved/edited</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Articles Published</strong></td><td>How many articles the author moved to Published status</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Words Written</strong></td><td>Estimated total words written across all articles (approximate)</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Avg Quality Score</strong></td><td>The average AI Optimizer score across all the author's articles</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Last Active</strong></td><td>When the author last made a documentation contribution</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<h2>Time Period Filter</h2>
<p>Use the date range picker to choose the period you want to analyze:</p>
<ul>
<li>Last 7 days</li>
<li>Last 30 days</li>
<li>Last 90 days</li>
<li>Custom date range</li>
</ul>

<h2>Activity Heatmap</h2>
<p>Above the stats table, the Activity Heatmap shows a GitHub-style contribution graph — one square per day, colored by activity level. This visualizes team activity patterns across the year, making it easy to spot:</p>
<ul>
<li>High-output sprints (dark squares)</li>
<li>Documentation droughts (empty weeks)</li>
<li>Individual contributor patterns (use the team member filter)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Contribution Timeline</h2>
<p>Below the heatmap, the Contribution Timeline shows a chronological list of recent documentation contributions — who created or updated which article and when. This gives context to the summary numbers by showing the actual work in sequence.</p>

<h2>Using This Data</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>In 1:1s</strong>: Review an individual's output trends and quality scores over time</li>
<li><strong>Sprint planning</strong>: See who has capacity (low recent output) and who is fully loaded</li>
<li><strong>Recognition</strong>: Identify top contributors and acknowledge their work</li>
<li><strong>Quality gaps</strong>: Authors with low average quality scores may benefit from training or support</li>
<li><strong>Documentation debt</strong>: Long stretches of no activity mean your KB might be falling behind product changes</li>
</ul>
