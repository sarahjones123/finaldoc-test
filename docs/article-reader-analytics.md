<h1>Article and Reader Analytics</h1>
<p>The Article Analytics view gives you per-article performance data — which articles are most read, how long readers spend on them, and what feedback they leave. The Reader Analytics view (available when reader authentication is on) gives you per-reader data.</p>

<h2>Article Analytics</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Article &amp; Reader Analytics</strong></li>
</ol>

<h3>Article Performance Table</h3>
<p>The table shows every published article with:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Article title and category</strong></li>
<li><strong>Total views</strong> — Times the article was opened in the selected date range</li>
<li><strong>Unique readers</strong> — Distinct visitors who read the article</li>
<li><strong>Average time on page</strong> — How long readers spend on average</li>
<li><strong>Feedback score</strong> — Average helpfulness rating (thumbs up/down ratio)</li>
<li><strong>Last updated</strong> — When the article was last edited</li>
</ul>

<h3>Article Detail View</h3>
<p>Click any article row to see its individual analytics dashboard:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Views over time</strong> — A chart showing daily view counts</li>
<li><strong>Traffic sources</strong> — Where readers came from (direct, search, internal KB navigation, external link)</li>
<li><strong>Search queries that led here</strong> — Which search terms resulted in readers clicking this article</li>
<li><strong>Exit rate</strong> — What percentage of readers left the KB after reading this article (vs. continuing to another article)</li>
<li><strong>Reader feedback</strong> — All individual feedback comments left on this article</li>
</ul>

<h2>Reader Analytics (Requires Reader Authentication)</h2>
<p>When readers are required to log in, you can see individual reader data:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Reader list</strong> — All readers with their last activity date and total articles read</li>
<li><strong>Per-reader view</strong> — Click any reader to see every article they have read, when, and how long they spent</li>
<li><strong>Reading progress</strong> — For readers working through structured onboarding content, see their completion percentage</li>
</ul>
<p>This data is useful for customer success teams to verify that customers have read onboarding materials, or to proactively reach out to customers who have not accessed certain resources.</p>

<h2>Sorting and Filtering</h2>
<p>Sort the article analytics table by any column — most viewed, highest rated, most recently updated, or lowest feedback score (to find articles that need improvement). Filter by date range, category, or article status.</p>

<h2>Exporting</h2>
<p>Click <strong>Export CSV</strong> to download the analytics data as a spreadsheet. The CSV includes all visible columns for all articles in the current filter.</p>
