<h1>Reader Experience Overview</h1>
<p>The public knowledge base is what your readers see when they visit your documentation. FinalDoc delivers a clean, fast, and feature-rich reading experience. This article explains what readers see and how to configure it.</p>

<h2>What Readers See</h2>
<p>When a reader visits your knowledge base URL (e.g., <code>https://yourproject.finaldoc.io</code>), they see:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Header</strong> — Your project name, logo, and navigation links</li>
<li><strong>Search bar</strong> — A prominent search box to find articles instantly</li>
<li><strong>Category listing</strong> — All published categories and articles, organized by your defined structure</li>
<li><strong>Featured articles</strong> — Any articles you have marked as featured appear prominently on the homepage</li>
<li><strong>AI assistant button</strong> — The Ved AI chatbot (if enabled) — a floating button that opens a chat interface</li>
</ul>

<h2>Article Pages</h2>
<p>When a reader opens an article, they see:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Article title</strong> — In large, clear heading format</li>
<li><strong>Author and date</strong> — Who wrote it and when it was last updated</li>
<li><strong>Reading time estimate</strong> — How long the article takes to read</li>
<li><strong>Table of contents</strong> — Auto-generated from H2 and H3 headings (if the article is long enough)</li>
<li><strong>Article body</strong> — The full formatted content with images, code blocks, tables, and more</li>
<li><strong>Tags</strong> — Clickable tags that show related articles</li>
<li><strong>Feedback section</strong> — "Was this article helpful?" thumbs up/down + optional comment</li>
<li><strong>Reactions</strong> — Emoji reactions (if enabled) — readers can react with 👍 ❤️ 👏 🤔 🚀</li>
<li><strong>Reading progress bar</strong> — A thin bar at the top of the screen showing how far through the article the reader is</li>
<li><strong>Listen button</strong> — Audio playback of the article using AI text-to-speech (if enabled)</li>
<li><strong>Related articles</strong> — AI-suggested articles related to the current one</li>
</ul>

<h2>Navigation</h2>
<p>Readers navigate through your KB using:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Left sidebar</strong> — Category and article tree. Expandable categories with article links. Always visible on desktop</li>
<li><strong>Breadcrumbs</strong> — Shows the path from home to the current article (e.g., Home → Category → Sub-category → Article)</li>
<li><strong>Table of contents</strong> — Jump to any section of a long article with a click</li>
<li><strong>Search</strong> — Instantly searches all published articles. Results rank by relevance using semantic AI search</li>
<li><strong>Keyboard navigation</strong> — Readers can navigate with arrow keys when focused on the search bar</li>
</ul>

<h2>Reader Login (Private KB)</h2>
<p>If your knowledge base requires login (or has mixed visibility with some private content), readers will see a login page before accessing restricted content. The login page is fully customizable with your brand colors, logo, and welcome message via <strong>Settings → Appearance → Portal Builder → Login Page</strong>.</p>

<h2>Dark Mode</h2>
<p>The public KB respects the reader's operating system preference — if their OS is in dark mode, the KB automatically shows in dark mode. Readers can also manually toggle dark/light mode using a button in the KB header.</p>

<h2>Mobile Experience</h2>
<p>FinalDoc's public KB is fully responsive and works on phones and tablets:</p>
<ul>
<li>The sidebar collapses into a hamburger menu on small screens</li>
<li>The table of contents collapses into a "jump to section" button</li>
<li>All content scales and reflows properly</li>
<li>The search bar is always accessible</li>
</ul>

<h2>Language Selector</h2>
<p>If you have published articles in multiple languages (using the Translation feature), readers see a language selector dropdown in the article header to switch between language versions.</p>

<h2>Offline Reading (PWA)</h2>
<p>FinalDoc is a Progressive Web App (PWA). Readers can install it on their device and read previously viewed articles even when offline. An "Add to Home Screen" prompt may appear on mobile devices. See the <strong>Offline Reading (PWA)</strong> article for details.</p>
