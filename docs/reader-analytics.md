<h1>Reader Analytics</h1>
<p>Reader Analytics shows who is reading your knowledge base — which readers are most active, what articles they're reading, how long they spend, and where they're coming from. This helps you understand your actual audience and identify VIP readers who rely heavily on your KB.</p>

<h2>Opening Reader Analytics</h2>
<ol>
<li>Click <strong>Analytics</strong> in the left sidebar</li>
<li>Click <strong>Article &amp; Reader Analytics</strong></li>
<li>Click the <strong>Readers</strong> tab at the top</li>
</ol>

<h2>Reader Table</h2>
<p>The main table shows every reader who has visited your knowledge base:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Reader</strong> — Name and email (if the reader is logged in). Anonymous readers show as "Anonymous Visitor"</li>
<li><strong>Articles read</strong> — Total distinct articles viewed</li>
<li><strong>Total sessions</strong> — Number of separate visits to the KB</li>
<li><strong>Total time</strong> — Cumulative time spent reading</li>
<li><strong>First visit</strong> — When they first came to the KB</li>
<li><strong>Last visit</strong> — Most recent visit date</li>
<li><strong>Country</strong> — Geographic location based on IP</li>
</ul>

<h2>Reader Detail View</h2>
<p>Click any reader row to open their detail view:</p>
<ul>
<li>A timeline of every article they've visited</li>
<li>Search queries they've made</li>
<li>Feedback they've submitted</li>
<li>Their chatbot conversation history (if any)</li>
</ul>
<p>This is useful for understanding the customer journey — what do customers look at before contacting support?</p>

<h2>Anonymous vs. Logged-In Readers</h2>
<p>Readers who haven't logged in are tracked by session/cookie. Their data is grouped by session — multiple sessions from the same person appear as separate anonymous visitors. Logged-in readers are tracked across sessions by their account, giving a complete picture of their engagement.</p>

<h2>Filtering Options</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Date range</strong> — Focus on a specific time period</li>
<li><strong>Country</strong> — Filter by geographic region</li>
<li><strong>Reader group</strong> — Filter to a specific access group (e.g., "Enterprise customers")</li>
<li><strong>Logged in / Anonymous</strong> — Toggle to see only authenticated or anonymous readers</li>
</ul>

<h2>Privacy Considerations</h2>
<p>Reader analytics are covered by your privacy policy. If your KB is public, inform readers in your privacy policy that you track page views and reading activity. If readers are logged in, they have consented to this as part of their account creation. Anonymous reader data does not include any personally identifiable information beyond approximate geographic location.</p>
