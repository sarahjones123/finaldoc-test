<h1>AI Auto-Draft from Content Gaps</h1>
<p>FinalDoc can automatically generate draft articles to fill gaps in your knowledge base — topics readers are searching for but you haven't documented yet. Instead of staring at a blank page, the AI gives you a complete first draft based on the search query or topic.</p>

<h2>Triggering an Auto-Draft</h2>
<p>Auto-drafts can be triggered from several places:</p>

<h3>From Search Analytics (Content Gaps)</h3>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Analytics → Search Analytics &amp; Content Gap Analysis</strong></li>
<li>Click the <strong>Content Gaps</strong> tab</li>
<li>Find a zero-result search query you want to create content for</li>
<li>Click <strong>AI Auto-Draft</strong> next to the query</li>
<li>FinalDoc generates a draft article on this topic</li>
</ol>

<h3>From Ticket Analysis Recommendations</h3>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Ticket Analysis → Recommendations</strong></li>
<li>Find a "Create New Article" recommendation</li>
<li>Click <strong>Create Draft</strong></li>
<li>The AI generates a draft based on the ticket analysis findings for this topic</li>
</ol>

<h3>From the New Article Dialog</h3>
<ol>
<li>Go to <strong>Documentation</strong></li>
<li>Click <strong>+ New Article</strong></li>
<li>Click <strong>Generate with AI</strong></li>
<li>Enter the topic or question</li>
<li>Click <strong>Generate Draft</strong></li>
</ol>

<h2>What the AI Generates</h2>
<p>Auto-drafts include:</p>
<ul>
<li>A descriptive article title based on the topic</li>
<li>An introduction explaining what the article covers</li>
<li>H2 sections covering the key aspects of the topic</li>
<li>Step-by-step instructions where relevant</li>
<li>A brief conclusion or next steps section</li>
</ul>
<p>The draft is written in general terms — it doesn't know the specific details of your product. You <strong>must review and edit the draft</strong> to add your product-specific details, correct any inaccuracies, and fill in specific steps.</p>

<h2>Reviewing and Publishing Auto-Drafts</h2>
<ol>
<li>Auto-drafts are created as <strong>Draft</strong> status — never published automatically</li>
<li>Open the draft in the Documentation editor</li>
<li>Read through it carefully</li>
<li>Replace placeholder content with actual product instructions</li>
<li>Add screenshots where needed</li>
<li>Run the AI Optimizer to check quality before publishing</li>
<li>Publish when satisfied</li>
</ol>

<h2>AI Draft Quality</h2>
<p>Auto-drafts are a starting point, not a finished product. They save you the blank-page problem and give you a solid outline, but they cannot replace your product expertise. Treat them as a first draft that needs 20–40 minutes of editing to become a quality article.</p>

<h2>Finding Auto-Drafts Later</h2>
<p>Auto-generated drafts appear in the Documentation view with a special "AI Draft" badge. Filter by <strong>Status: Draft</strong> and look for the badge to find unreviewed auto-drafts.</p>
